# Habit Formation Framework

HabitForge uses a structured model for designing habits.

---

## Step 1 � Cue

A trigger that initiates the behavior.

Examples include:

scheduled reminders  
calendar integration  
environmental prompts

---

## Step 2 � Behavior

The desired action performed by the user.

Examples include:

practicing music  
completing a lesson  
reviewing learning material

---

## Step 3 � Reward

Positive feedback that reinforces the behavior.

Examples include:

progress indicators  
achievement badges  
skill improvements

---

# Habit Design Principles

Several principles guide HabitForge systems.

---

## Principle 1 � Start Small

Habits succeed when the starting action is simple.

A small daily action is easier to sustain.

---

## Principle 2 � Reduce Friction

The fewer steps required to start the behavior, the more likely it will occur.

---

## Principle 3 � Visible Progress

Users remain motivated when they see improvement.

Examples include:

streak counters  
progress bars  
achievement levels

---

## Principle 4 � Identity Reinforcement

Habits strengthen when users see themselves as the type of person who performs the behavior.

Products should reinforce positive identity.

---
