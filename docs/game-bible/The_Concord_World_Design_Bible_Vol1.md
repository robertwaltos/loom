# THE CONCORD — WORLD DESIGN BIBLE
## Volume I · The Twenty Launch Worlds · World Generation Framework
## Classification: Design Canon · v1.0

---

> Every world in The Concord is a character. It has a wound, a gift, a history, and a question.
> The physical properties are real (stellar classification, atmospheric composition, orbital mechanics).
> The cultural and political properties emerge from NPC populations that have been running since Year 1.
> No two worlds are the same because no two histories are the same.

---

## WORLD GENERATION FRAMEWORK

### The Five Properties of Every World

**1. Stellar Class** (determines KALON issuance multiplier)
- G-class (Sun-like): ×1.0 base · High habitability · Highest population potential
- K-class (Orange dwarf): ×0.85 · Excellent long-term stability · Favoured by Returnists
- M-class (Red dwarf): ×0.6 · Tidal locking risk · Low luminosity · Niche populations
- F-class (Yellow-white): ×1.2 · Higher radiation · Shorter star lifespan · Risk factor
- Binary systems: ×1.4 (doubled, with instability modifier) · Complex politics
- Anomalous (unclassified): ×0 to ×2.0 · Only 12 exist in the 600 · No two alike

**2. Node Density** (1–10, affects issuance multiplier directly)
- Measures the concentration of naturally-occurring Lattice resonance points
- Higher density = easier Lattice integration, higher KALON issuance
- Density cannot be increased artificially (the one thing KALON cannot buy)
- The three highest-density worlds in the network were excluded from the launch set
  (see Founding Wound 2 — the excluded worlds are publicly known, their density scores are public)

**3. Lattice Integrity** (0–100%, affects issuance, degrades under Ascendancy interference)
- Starts at 100% for all newly opened worlds
- Degrades 0.1–0.5% per incident of frequency interference
- Floor of 10% (complete severance requires physical destruction of the node hardware)
- Restoration: 1–3 years of maintenance investment, expensive, requires specialist crews
- Worlds with integrity below 40% show visible effects: Lattice transit instability, communication delays

**4. Population Density** (NPC count, determines productivity index)
- Inhabited worlds range from 50,000 to 8 billion NPC inhabitants
- Population affects world productivity index ±20% (multiplies issuance)
- Population is not static — grows or shrinks based on conditions, governance, events
- The Architect tracks every NPC population count. Always has.

**5. Historical State Hash** (quarterly-generated, affects probability tables)
- Derived from: Chronicle entries involving this world, weighted by phase multiplier
- High negative valence hash → increased probability of crisis events, NPC unrest
- High positive valence hash → increased Survey Corps interest, trade route formation
- Hash is public. The formula for generating it from Chronicle data is published.
  Players can calculate it themselves. Some have built tracking tools.

## THE TWENTY LAUNCH WORLDS

### WORLD 01 — ALKAHEST
**Stellar Class:** G2V (near-solar analogue) · **Node Density:** 8 · **Population:** 2.1 billion NPC
**Coordinates:** Survey Reference 001-G · First Lock: Year 0 (Founding Event World)
**Sovereignty History:** Okafor-Nwosu Foundation → Assembly Common Trust (Year 12) → Current: Twelve-Dynasty Compact
**KALON Issuance (Year 105):** ~52M/year · **Lattice Integrity:** 97%
**Founding Wound Connection:** World 1 is where the door opened. Amara Okafor-Nwosu's house with the green roof is on World 1. The original Lattice node hardware — the physical equipment from the 2031 Lagos basement, transported here in Year 3 in a ceremony that was simultaneously a scientific preservation project and a deeply contested political act — is here under a climate-controlled preservation facility.

**Character:** The most historically significant and most politically complicated world in the Concord. Every major institution has an office here. Every major dynasty considers a presence here strategically important. The NPC population, after 105 years, has the particular quality of people who live at the intersection of too much significance — simultaneously proud of their world's history and exhausted by its weight.

**The Specific History That Matters:** In Year 12, a property dispute arose over approximately 40 square kilometres surrounding the original Lattice node site. Seven dynasties filed competing sovereignty claims. The dispute went to Assembly arbitration for 4 years and produced a 340-page ruling that established the legal precedent for "historically significant site" designation — a category that now covers 23 locations across the 600 worlds, each with its own specific governance structure. The 340-page ruling is in the Chronicle. Legal scholars have been arguing about its implications since Year 16. Nobody has read all 340 pages. The Architect has.

**The Thing That Happens Here:** Every five years, on the anniversary of the Founding Event, World 1 becomes the site of the Concord's largest public gathering. Players and NPC alike. Chronicle entries filed from World 1 on that anniversary receive a 2× multiplier for the Founding Era phase bonus retroactively — the one exception to the "phase multipliers are fixed at time of entry" rule. This exception is documented in the Permanence Covenant. It has been used exactly once, by a dynasty that timed their Year 50 filing to coincide with the 50th anniversary celebration.

### WORLD 02 — MERIDIAN'S REST
**Stellar Class:** K1V · **Node Density:** 9 · **Population:** 890M NPC
**Survey Year:** Year 0 · **Founding Family Sovereignty:** House of Vael-Okafor (Claimable Player Dynasty)
**KALON Issuance (Year 105):** ~48M/year · **Lattice Integrity:** 94%
**Founding Wound Connection:** World 2 was assigned to House Vael-Okafor before the Assembly existed. It is one of the three highest-node-density worlds in the launch set. The fact that it went to a founding family — rather than being held in common trust — is in the public record. The justification in the record is "exploratory services rendered." Players who look up what services those were will find a 3-line entry in a Year 0 pre-Chronicle institutional log.

**Character:** Beautiful and aware of it. World 2 has the kind of climate that makes people want to stay, and the kind of political history that has made people want to leave, and the combination produces a culture that is simultaneously hospitable and historically exhausted. The NPC population produces some of the finest Chronicle writers in the Concord — a disproportionate number of the most-referenced personal entries come from World 2 inhabitants — which scholars argue is either a coincidence or a product of living inside a wound for a hundred years.

**The Vael-Okafor Dynasty (Player-Claimable):** This is the only founding family dynasty that can be claimed by a player. The House of Vael-Okafor is the political descendant of the founding family that received World 2. The dynasty's Chronicle extends from Year 0. Its earliest entries include primary source documentation of the pre-Assembly period that exists nowhere else in public records. Claiming this dynasty means inheriting: extraordinary historical Chronicle depth, significant initial KALON reserves, World 2 sovereignty complications, and the particular challenge of operating a dynasty that every other player knows has advantages they find questionable.

The dynasty has been without a sovereign player since Year 89 (the last player departed under circumstances that are in the Chronicle). It is currently administered by a Tier 3 NPC regent. The regency ends the moment a player claims it.

### WORLD 03 — THE AMBER REACH
**Stellar Class:** G5V · **Node Density:** 7 · **Population:** 3.4 billion NPC
**Survey Year:** Year 0 · **Sovereignty History:** Commonwealth Trust from Year 0
**KALON Issuance (Year 105):** ~45M/year · **Lattice Integrity:** 99%

The most populous of the launch worlds and the most aggressively democratic. The Amber Reach's NPC population has a culture of civic engagement that the Architect once described, in a non-public notation, as "the most effective counter to the Continuationist political model currently operating in the Concord." It meant this as an observation, not an endorsement.

The planetary government has changed form four times in 105 years — from the original colonial administrative structure to an emergent direct-democracy system that processes approximately 4 million assembly votes per year from its NPC population alone. Players who hold sovereignty on The Amber Reach discover very quickly that "sovereignty" here means something different than it means on most worlds. You can hold the legal designation. You cannot hold the population. The Amber Reach's NPC inhabitants have been filing Chronicles about their own governance for 105 years. The collective Chronicle depth of World 3's indigenous NPC population is higher than many player dynasties.

**The Historical Curiosity:** In Year 38, the World 3 NPC population held an internal referendum on whether to formally petition the Assembly to reclassify their world as a "Sovereign NPC State" with its own Assembly representation. The referendum passed. The petition was filed. The Architect produced a 200-page analysis. The Assembly voted on it in Year 43 and the motion failed, 61-39. The 200-page Architect analysis is in the Chronicle. The 39 Assembly members who voted yes are named. Several of their dynasties still hold Assembly seats.

### WORLD 04 — IRON MERIDIAN
**Stellar Class:** F8V · **Node Density:** 5 · **Population:** 450M NPC
**Survey Year:** Year 0 · **Sovereignty History:** Industrial Consortium Compact
**KALON Issuance (Year 105):** ~29M/year · **Lattice Integrity:** 88%

The Concord's primary heavy industrial world is a deliberate choice — it was selected for the launch set specifically because its atmospheric conditions support manufacturing infrastructure that would be difficult to establish on more habitableworlds. It is not beautiful. The NPC population knows it is not beautiful. They are proud of this in the way that people are proud of things that have cost them something.

**The Integrity Dip to 88%:** In Year 87, Iron Meridian's Lattice node experienced a 12-hour frequency interference incident that Concord technical teams attributed to Ascendancy activity. The incident coincided with an ongoing Assembly debate about mandatory maintenance levies for industrial worlds. The 12% integrity loss was repaired over 3 years at significant expense. The repair cost was financed partly from the Industrial Consortium's KALON reserves and partly from the Commons Fund. The debate about whether industrial worlds should bear different levy obligations than habitable worlds has not been resolved. It is currently in the second year of a "technical review" process that has been awaiting report for 6 years.

### WORLD 05 — SELENE'S CRADLE
**Stellar Class:** K3V · **Node Density:** 6 · **Population:** 1.2 billion NPC
**Survey Year:** Year 0 · **Sovereignty History:** Lattice Covenant Administered (Year 15 forward)
**KALON Issuance (Year 105):** ~36M/year · **Lattice Integrity:** 100%

The only launch world under Lattice Covenant administration, and the world that the Covenant points to when explaining why it should be trusted with administrative authority. In 90 years of Covenant stewardship, World 5's Lattice integrity has never dropped below 100%. Its NPC population is, by measurable metrics, healthier and more Chronicle-active than comparable worlds. Its political governance is participatory and consistent. It has never missed a levy payment. It has never filed an emergency infrastructure request.

The Continuationists note that World 5 is also the world with the highest percentage of Covenant membership in its NPC population, and that this correlation may explain the outcomes rather than the governance model. The Covenant notes that causation runs in both directions and that both things can be true. The Architect has modelled this correlation 14 times. Its conclusion, in a non-public notation: *"The Covenant's model works on World 5. Whether it scales is a different question. I do not know the answer. I continue to observe."*

### WORLD 06 — THE VEIL OF KASS
**Stellar Class:** Binary K2/M4 · **Node Density:** 8 · **Population:** 620M NPC
**Survey Year:** Year 0 · **Sovereignty History:** Contested · Seven ownership transitions in 105 years
**KALON Issuance (Year 105):** ~58M/year · **Lattice Integrity:** 82%

The most politically volatile of the launch worlds and the most economically productive. Binary stellar systems produce complex Lattice geometries — the node network on World 6 interfaces with both stars' gravitational fields, producing a KALON issuance multiplier that no single-star world can match. The price of this productivity is instability: the Lattice geometry shifts quarterly in ways that require constant maintenance recalibration, which is expensive and technically demanding, and which creates a continuous point of conflict between whoever holds sovereignty (and is expected to fund maintenance) and whoever wants to hold sovereignty next.

The seven sovereignty transitions in 105 years have each been peaceful and each has followed the same pattern: the incoming dynasty promises to solve the maintenance funding problem, spends 10-15 years discovering that it cannot, and transfers sovereignty to someone who promises the same thing. The Architect's private assessment, prepared in Year 67 and not published: *"World 6 will not be stably governed until the Assembly accepts that its maintenance costs are a common infrastructure expense, not a sovereignty responsibility. I expect this recognition will come in approximately Year 140–160, after one more transition cycle."*

### WORLD 07 — DEEP TIDAL
**Stellar Class:** M2V · **Node Density:** 4 · **Population:** 89M NPC
**Survey Year:** Year 0 · **Sovereignty History:** Survey Corps Administration (permanent)
**KALON Issuance (Year 105):** ~14M/year · **Lattice Integrity:** 96%

The only permanently Survey Corps administered world in the launch set — it is both a functional world and a Survey Corps operational base. Tidal locking around its M-dwarf star means one hemisphere is perpetually day and one perpetually night, producing a distinct NPC culture: the Day Side populations are agricultural and outward-facing; the Night Side populations are industrial and inward-facing; the Twilight Belt — a 200-kilometre-wide strip of perpetual dawn and dusk — is the most valuable real estate in the known network.

The NPC population of the Twilight Belt has produced, over 105 years, a disproportionate number of Survey Corps officers, Lattice physicists, and Lattice Covenant theologians. The prevailing theory among researchers who study this pattern is that living permanently in the transitional space between two extremes produces people with a particular facility for holding contradiction. Brother Ekundayo Manu is from the Twilight Belt.

### WORLD 08 — VARANTHA STATION
**Stellar Class:** G8V · **Node Density:** 7 · **Population:** 340M NPC · **Plus: 2.3M transient**
**Survey Year:** Year 0 · **Sovereignty History:** Free Port Compact from Year 8
**KALON Issuance (Year 105):** ~38M/year · **Lattice Integrity:** 93%

World 8 is the Concord's primary commercial hub, which means it is simultaneously the place where the most KALON changes hands and the place where the Concord's governance principles are most visibly under pressure. The Free Port Compact — an agreement between 23 trading dynasties, the Assembly Commerce Committee, and (quietly) three institutions whose names are in the agreement but who requested they be listed as "institutional signatories" rather than "parties" — established a special regulatory zone that has different levy and audit requirements than standard world governance.

Dagna Thorvaldsen-Mbeki has been auditing the Free Port Compact's compliance since Year 40. Her public reports are thorough and accurate. Her three classified reports include one specifically about World 8. Players who obtain Assembly committee access through sufficient voting weight and the right procedural knowledge can request access to the declassified portions of this report. The declassified portions are 34% of the total. The other 66% is described in the index as: *"Omitted per Assembly Resolution 88-7: Systemic Vulnerability Information."* The index was itself inadvertently made public in Year 92, which means players can see what they cannot read.

### WORLDS 09–20 · SUMMARY PROFILES

**World 09 — AURUM DEEPS:** K0V, Node 6, 1.8B NPC. Agricultural surplus world. Feeds 14 other worlds. Sovereignty is therefore always contested but never violently — nobody wants to disrupt the supply chain. The Chronicle entries from World 9 NPC farmers are the most quietly philosophical in the entire network.

**World 10 — THE GREY ACCORD:** G3V, Node 7, 770M NPC. Medical research primary world. Life extension technology is primarily researched here. The irony that the world studying longevity has a higher NPC mortality rate than most comparable worlds — due to the research ethics involved — is noted in the Chronicle and discussed in Assembly approximately every 15 years.

**World 11 — REDSHIFT HAVEN:** F9V, Node 5, 520M NPC. High-radiation world, extreme infrastructure requirements. The NPC population has developed a cultural relationship to risk that the rest of the Concord finds either admirable or alarming depending on who is doing the finding. Their Chronicle writing style is distinctive: brief, declarative, no unnecessary words.

**World 12 — THE PALE CIRCUIT:** G6V, Node 9, 1.1B NPC. Highest-density node world in the launch set. This is not coincidence — it was selected specifically for this property. The implication (that a higher-density world was available but not selected, which it was — three of them) is in the public record for anyone willing to compare the launch world selection criteria against the excluded candidate survey data.

**World 13 — ORIN'S MARGIN:** K2V, Node 5, 430M NPC. Named after a Survey Corps officer who died during the approach survey — the only world in the launch set named for someone who died opening it. The NPC culture has built a specific tradition around mortality and memory that predates the Lattice Covenant and is distinct from it.

**World 14 — THE BONE CHORUS:** M3V, Node 3, 98M NPC. Night-side dominant world. The NPC population developed acoustic communication technologies before the Lattice arrived — complex sound-based languages that encode information in harmonics. The Lattice Covenant considers this significant.

**World 15 — CINNABAR REACH:** G7V, Node 7, 2.3B NPC. Culturally the most diverse of the launch worlds — the original colonisation protocol brought populations from 14 different Earth regions specifically to test governance models under diversity conditions. 105 years later, the experiment is ongoing and the results are complex. World 15 has the most active Assembly representation debates of any world.

**World 16 — THALASSA DEEP:** K4V, Node 6, 1.6B NPC. Primarily ocean world. 8% land surface. The NPC population lives primarily in coastal and floating city infrastructure. The Lattice node is underwater, which creates unique maintenance challenges and has produced the Concord's only fully submarine Survey Corps support base.

**World 17 — THE RESONANT PLAIN:** G4V, Node 8, 890M NPC. The world where Lattice resonance is most physically perceptible to NPC (and player) inhabitants — proximity to high-density nodes produces a low-frequency hum audible to most species. The NPC culture has built music around this frequency for 105 years. The Lattice Covenant claims this as evidence. The Architect has no public comment on this claim.

**World 18 — IRONSHORE:** F7V, Node 4, 280M NPC. Mining technology development world. Matterwave synthesis made traditional mining economically irrelevant by Year 40, but World 18 continued developing the technology because Lattice-integrated geological survey tools that emerged from mining research turned out to be extraordinarily valuable for Survey Corps path-finding. The world reinvented itself in 25 years. The Chronicle of that reinvention is required reading in Survey Corps training.

**World 19 — THE WATCHER:** G9V, Node 6, 720M NPC. Telescope array world — the Concord's primary deep-space observation infrastructure is here. The scientists of World 19 are the first to see everything that happens in the outer survey arc. They are also constrained by the most comprehensive non-disclosure protocols of any research institution in the Concord. The NPC population of World 19 has strong opinions about this.

**World 20 — ALKAHEST'S MIRROR:** K5V, Node 5, 310M NPC. The last of the launch worlds and the most recently politically contested. World 20 was, in Year 0, the world with the least interest — low node density, marginal habitability, distant from the primary survey arcs. By Year 50, it had become the location of the Concord's primary diplomatic corps headquarters, for reasons that are complex, historical, and include a property dispute, a compromise, and one very long negotiation that produced an outcome nobody originally wanted and that has turned out to work better than what they did want.

## WORLD GENERATION: WORLDS 21–600

### The Survey Arc System

The 600 worlds are organised into six radial survey arcs from the original Lattice hub at World 1. Each arc covers approximately 100 worlds. The arcs are:

- **Arc Okafor:** Worlds 21–100 (primary arc, original survey direction)
- **Arc Adeyemi:** Worlds 101–200 (named in Year 45 after Assembly vote, contested)
- **Arc Meridian:** Worlds 201–300 (mid-distance arc, contains Adhan's Passage at World 247)
- **Arc Vael:** Worlds 301–400 (named for Commodore Vael in Year 62 at her retirement)
- **Arc Deep:** Worlds 401–500 (outer arc, increasing anomaly frequency)
- **Arc Final:** Worlds 501–600 (the outermost, unmapped beyond World 510 as of Year 105)

### The World Personality Matrix

Each world has a generated personality derived from seven parameters:

1. **Settlement Pattern:** Colonial (humans from Earth), Indigenous (pre-existing NPC civilisation), Mixed, Emergent (no settlement history, NPC population developed after Lattice access)

2. **Cultural Dominant:** Varies by world — 23 cultural archetypes currently modelled, each with distinct Chronicle writing styles, governance preferences, economic behaviours

3. **Historical Trauma Index:** Derived from Chronicle entries, 0–100. Worlds above 60 have specific NPC behaviour modifications (reduced cooperation, increased Chronicle depth, specific economic restrictions)

4. **Ascendancy Exposure:** Distance from known Ascendancy activity patterns, integrity history, economic dependence on outer arc trade routes. Determines ambient threat level.

5. **Lattice Covenant Presence:** 0–95% of population (World 5 is highest). Affects NPC behaviour, Chronicle content, response to governance crises

6. **Returnist Sentiment:** 0–80%. Affects governance stability, levy compliance, Assembly vote patterns from NPC-derived representation pressures

7. **Survey Frontier Distance:** How far the world is from the advancing survey frontier. Affects economic opportunity, NPC psychology, player sovereignty value

## THE THREE EXCLUDED WORLDS

The public survey data for 2027's candidate world selection includes 54 evaluated candidates for the 20 launch slots. Three candidates with higher combined scores than several selected worlds were not included. Their survey data is in the Chronicle. Players can access it. The scoring discrepancy is visible.

**Candidate A (Survey Ref: EX-07):** G1V, Node 10, estimated population 3.2B. Node density 10 — the maximum possible. Excluded from the launch set. Current status: World 247 in Arc Meridian. Current governance: Primary Ascendancy inhabited world. Adhan's Passage.

**Candidate B (Survey Ref: EX-12):** G2V, Node 9, estimated population 1.8B. Currently World 312 — one of the worlds involved in the only Concord military engagement. Node 9 with full Lattice integration would produce approximately 90M KALON/year. Current issuance: 31M/year (Lattice integrity: 52%, result of the Year 62 engagement aftermath).

**Candidate C (Survey Ref: EX-31):** K0V, Node 9, estimated population 2.1B. Currently World 394. Sovereignty history: seven transfers in 105 years, currently under Assembly Common Trust administration pending a stability review that has been ongoing for 11 years. Its high-node infrastructure has never been fully developed because every sovereignty holder has prioritised short-term extraction over long-term investment. The Architect has a model of what World 394 would look like under stable governance. It has never been asked to share this model.
