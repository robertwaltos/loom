# THE CONCORD — GAME DESIGN BIBLE
## Canonical Design Reference · v1.0
## Classification: Design Canon · This Document Governs Mechanical Design Decisions

---

> The Concord is the synthesis of EVE Online's economic depth, WoW's social architecture,
> Path of Exile's rewarding complexity, Fortnite's seasonal world-change events, and
> Minecraft's ownership — all wrapped in the most ambitious narrative MMO ever designed:
> a 35-year civilization simulator where players collectively write history.
>
> Everything in this document serves a single design imperative:
> the player's choices must matter tomorrow, next year, and in twenty years.
> If an action leaves no permanent mark on the world, it should not exist.

---

## PART 1 — CORE DESIGN PHILOSOPHY

### What This Game Is

The Concord is a premium adult adventure MMO set during humanity's transition from scarcity to
the Age of Radiance. The game begins in the Disclosure Age (~2030) — the unstable, politically
explosive transition period when zero-point energy has dissolved the old economic foundations,
but the new ones are not yet built. Players arrive before the institutions are set, before the
precedents are established, before the 600 worlds are open.

This timing is not incidental. It is the entire point.

The Concord runs for 35 real years (2027–2062). It is not designed to be played forever — it is
designed to *be something*. At Year 105 (in-game), the 600th world opens and the civilisation
reaches its first reckoning. What happens after that is not designed. That is what the players
make of it.

### The Five Design Pillars

**1. Permanence.** Nothing resets. The Chronicle is permanent. Territory you hold is territory
you can lose. Dynasty legacy is real legacy. A lie told to an NPC in Year 1 is in their memory
in Year 30. The world does not accommodate you — you accommodate the world.

**2. Consequence at every timescale.** A 5-minute decision can echo for 20 years. The Assembly
vote you cast today is in the Chronicle forever. The wormhole node you build this month is
still there — or isn't — when your dynasty's grandchildren are playing.

**3. Complexity that rewards investment.** The depth of The Concord is not visible at character
creation. It emerges over months and years. Players who have been playing for 5 years know things
about the Chronicle, the Architect, the Sealed Chambers, and the Ascendancy's real origin that
players of 6 months cannot see yet. This is not gatekeeping — this is the game being actually
deep, not pretending to be.

**4. The social layer is the content.** Dynasties are the game's most important mechanic.
Political intrigue is not a feature bolted onto combat — it is the primary competitive
theatre. The Chronicle is not a journal — it is the game's social media platform. You do not
play alongside other players; you are entangled with them permanently.

**5. The world has its own logic.** The Stellar Standard formula produces real KALON economics.
The Lattice degrades under real load. NPC populations grow and shrink based on real conditions.
The Architect is not on the players' side and is not against them. The Ascendancy has a
legitimate origin that the Assembly will be forced to acknowledge. None of this was designed
for dramatic effect — it was designed because a world with its own logic produces better
stories than a world designed to flatter players.

### What This Game Is Not

The Concord is not a power fantasy. There is no endgame boss to kill. There is no level cap
at which you have won. There is no server reset that makes your losses temporary.

It is not a casual game. The depth is real. The investment required to understand Chronicle
Depth mechanics, KALON bond structures, MARKS cross-specialization, and the Sealed Chamber
conditions is substantial. This depth is a feature, not a barrier. The audience is players
who want a game that takes them seriously.

It is not a game that explains its secrets. The Architect's Five Private Conclusions are not
in the tutorial. The Sealed Chambers are not announced. The three Founding Wounds are not
explained in an onboarding document. Everything important is discovered, not told. This is
a standing design rule that cannot be overridden.

## PART 2 — THE CORE LOOP (MULTIPLE TIMESCALES)

The Concord operates at five nested timescales simultaneously. A player can engage at any
level on any given session. The loops compound — action in the 5-minute loop feeds the
daily loop feeds the seasonal loop feeds the era loop feeds the 35-year civilizational arc.

### The 5-Minute Loop

> Transit a Lattice node → discover something → act → Chronicle entry → consequence

Every Lattice transit is an opportunity. The network's 600 worlds are not static content
instances — they are living economies, political systems, and NPC societies. Transitioning
to a world means arriving somewhere with its own current state: ongoing political negotiations,
active trade imbalances, Survey Corps expeditions in progress, Ascendancy pressure on the
outer arc, or a Beacon anomaly that wasn't there last week.

**What a player can discover in 5 minutes:**
- A trade arbitrage opportunity between two worlds whose KALON rates have diverged
- A Survey Corps anomaly flagged in the stream but not yet investigated
- An NPC contact willing to share intelligence that the CID is actively suppressing
- A wormhole route instability creating a transit disruption that someone is going to
  make a Chronicle entry about — it should be you
- A minor political development in the Assembly that will matter in six months

**The Chronicle entry is not optional.** Acting without filing a Chronicle entry means
the action happened and left no record. The Chronicle is public by design. Filing entries
is how you build Chronicle Depth, which is how you build Assembly voting weight, which is
how you shape the civilization. Every entry is permanent. Write accordingly.

---

### The Daily Loop

> Monitor dynasty → respond to events → one meaningful decision → advance an investigation

**Dynasty monitoring (15–30 minutes):**
A player's dynasty holds territory across associated worlds. Each world has a current state:
KALON issuance rate, Lattice integrity, population productivity index, active political
relationships, and the Historical State Hash (publicly visible, formula published). Yesterday's
decisions are producing today's state. The dashboard is not an abstraction — it shows the
actual condition of actual places that NPCs live.

**The one meaningful decision:**
Every day, there is at least one decision that will matter in a year. Not a combat decision
(though those exist). A political decision: how to vote on the Assembly motion that closes
in 36 hours. An economic decision: whether to sell the ZPE surplus now or hold it as a hedge
against the Ascendancy-pressure-induced scarcity that your Intelligence MARK contact says is
coming. A diplomatic decision: whether to accept the non-aggression pact from the dynasty
that controls the three worlds between you and the outer arc.

These decisions are not clearly right or wrong. The game does not tell you what to do.
It provides information, Chronicle context, NPC relationships, and economic data. Then
it waits for you to decide. Then it remembers.

**Investigation advancement:**
The Concord's deep content lives in ongoing investigations that take weeks or months to
complete. Following the Kwame methodology fragments. Building the Adeyemi Chronicle
evidence chain. Mapping the Ascendancy's transit patterns in the outer arc. These are
not quest chains with waypoints — they are research projects that require Chronicle
search, NPC relationship building, and cross-referencing information from multiple sources.
The player advances them in the time available.

---

### The Session Loop (2–4 hours)

> Engage in one major activity: Survey Expedition, Assembly session, economic operation,
> diplomatic mission, or investigative deep-dive

This is the game's most fully-featured engagement layer. The content that requires
coordination with other players — Lattice Fault response events, Survey Expeditions into
uncharted space, Assembly floor debate on major motions — operates at this timescale.

A 3-hour Assembly session on a major constitutional motion is not unusual. A Survey
Expedition to an uncharted outer-arc cluster runs 2–5 hours. An economic operation to
corner the metamaterial market on a specific world cluster requires preparation and
execution over a full session. These are the activities that generate the Chronicle
entries that will still be read in 20 years.

---

### The Seasonal Loop (3–6 months real time)

> A global narrative thread emerges → players investigate → consequences are real →
> the world is permanently different

Each in-game "Year" (approximately 4 real months at the 3:1 compression ratio) is
a structured narrative arc — a Chronicle Season — with a global investigation thread
that all active players can contribute to. The Year's event has a name, a central
question, a set of discoverable facts, and a resolution that is determined by player
action.

**Chronicle Season structure:**
- **Month 1 — Signal:** Something changes. A Survey Corps anomaly. An Assembly motion
  that shouldn't exist. An NPC who stops talking. A KALON rate that doesn't make sense.
  The signal is visible in the data but not explained. Players who find it first have
  a Chronicle advantage.
- **Month 2 — Investigation:** The signal is now general knowledge. Multiple factions
  are pursuing competing interpretations. Intelligence MARK players are selling information.
  Academic MARK players are cross-referencing archives. The Architect is answering questions
  with answers that are completely true and deeply incomplete.
- **Month 3 — Convergence:** The competing interpretations cannot all be right. The Assembly
  is preparing a response motion. The evidence is almost fully assembled. The final Chronicle
  entries that will determine the outcome are being written now.
- **Resolution — Permanent Change:** The world changes. Physically, politically, economically.
  A world's KALON issuance shifts. A new Lattice route opens or closes. An NPC dies or
  decides something irreversible. The Chronicle Season's name is added to every item,
  Chronicle entry, and territory record created during it. You were there.

**Exclusive Season events:** Some seasonal events happen once. The first Void-Touched world
manifesting. The Silence Pattern. The 601st world signal. These are not repeatable content.
The players present when they occur carry that Chronicle credit permanently. This is intended.
The game is designed to have history that cannot be accessed retroactively. "I was there"
is a real achievement with real Chronicle weight.

---

### The Era Loop (7–20 real years)

> A civilizational phase concludes → new technologies and politics unlock →
> a Sealed Chamber opens → the game is fundamentally different

Five Eras structure the 35-year arc. Each era transition is permanent, irreversible, and
produced by player action collectively rather than by a timer:

**Era I — The Founding (Years 1–15 in-game / Real 2027–2032)**
Twenty worlds. Every player is within two degrees of every other player. The Chronicle is
young. Founding Marks are being awarded. The first Assembly sessions establish precedents
that will govern the civilization for 100 years. The Ascendancy does not yet exist as an
external threat — it is a political faction within the Assembly, and players who pay close
attention will see it beginning to separate.

Design principle: The early game should feel like being present at a founding. Every decision
is a precedent. Players in Era I are the civilization's grandparents.

**Era II — The Expansion (Years 16–40 in-game / Real 2032–2040)**
The world grows faster than its institutions. Survey Corps is opening 15–20 worlds per year.
KALON supply is expanding rapidly. The Assembly is becoming unwieldy. Wealth concentration
is visible in the Gini index. The first Continuationist/Returnist split formalises. The seeds
of what will become Ordinance 7 are being planted — players making decisions in Era II do not
know this yet.

**Era III — The Maturation (Years 41–70 in-game / Real 2041–2050)**
A civilization 40 real years old has weight. There are players who have been playing since
Year 1. The Chronicle has hundreds of millions of entries. The three Founding Wounds are
known but not resolved. The Architect's private conclusions are becoming pressure points.
The multigenerational design pays off here — players whose grandchildren are playing alongside
them.

**Era IV — The Reckoning (Years 71–95 in-game / Real 2051–2059)**
600 worlds are approaching full open. The Ascendancy controls territory equivalent to 85 worlds.
The Lattice degradation signal that Kwame Osei-Adeyemi predicted is becoming detectable by
anyone with the right instruments. The Assembly has three competing proposals for what to do
about it. None of them are fully correct. The choices made in Era I and II are producing
their outcomes.

**Era V — The Resolution (Years 96–105+ / Real 2060–2062+)**
This is not designed. This is what the players make of it.

## PART 3 — MECHANICS BORROWED AND ADAPTED

### From EVE Online — Player-Driven Economy With Real Stakes

The KALON system is entirely player-driven. There are no NPC price floors. Supply and demand
are real. Economic warfare is possible and intended.

**The KALON formula:**
```
World KALON Issuance = Stellar Class Multiplier × Node Density Score × Lattice Integrity × Population Index
```

Each component is publicly visible. Players can calculate expected issuance for any world.
This transparency is intentional — it creates informed market actors, which creates real
market dynamics.

**Wormhole routes as owned infrastructure:**
Discovering a stable wormhole route between two worlds and registering the claim with the
Assembly creates a legal toll-collection right. The claiming dynasty can charge transit fees,
set terms for allied use, and — critically — can lose the route to physical attack. A route
you have spent three years building and monetizing can be severed by an Ascendancy operation
or a hostile dynasty with the right military capability. This loss is genuine and irreversible.
It is supposed to hurt. EVE Online taught us that only losses that hurt create meaningful wins.

**Industrial chains:**
ZPE extraction plants → metamaterial manufacturing → Lattice node construction components →
world development infrastructure. Every step in the chain requires a different MARKS
specialization to operate optimally. No single dynasty can vertically integrate all of it
efficiently. The economy requires interdependence.

**Market manipulation:**
Cornering the rare metamaterial market for a specific world type is possible. Economic
warfare between dynasties and factions is possible. Spreading false intelligence about
KALON issuance rates on the Chronicle grey market is possible. All of it is recorded.
All of it is permanent.

**Espionage:**
Intelligence MARK players can infiltrate factions, access private Assembly briefings,
steal economic research, and sell it on the Chronicle grey market. The buyer does not
know if what they're buying is accurate until they act on it. This is intended. The
grey market Chronicle entry is permanent whether or not the intelligence was real.

---

### From WoW — Dynasty Social Architecture

Dynasties are The Concord's guilds, rebuilt from the ground up for permanent consequence.

**Dynasty structure:**
Every dynasty has a Charter, a Chronicle, a Reserve (shared economy), and a hierarchy of
offices with real mechanical authority. The Trade Minister controls the dynasty's market
access terms. The Survey Commander can unlock new world route registrations. The Chronicle
Archivist controls who can file entries under the dynasty banner with what weight.

**Dynastic offices create real political power:**
These are not cosmetic titles. The Assembly's Chronicle Depth calculation weights entries
by the author's office within their dynasty. A Trade Minister's filing about market
conditions carries more Assembly weight than a new member's equivalent filing. Dynasty
politics are therefore consequential — the inner circle fight is about real mechanical power.

**Dynasty legacy:**
When a dynasty's active membership falls to zero and remains there for 90 days, the dynasty
enters Completion status. Its Chronicle becomes a curated permanent archive. NPC scholars
reference it. The Architect reads it. Subsequent player characters in its territory
encounter references to it. Dynasty Completion is not failure — it is a different kind
of legacy, one that some dynasties deliberately choose.

**Inter-dynasty diplomacy:**
All formal diplomatic agreements — alliances, non-aggression pacts, trade treaties, joint
Survey operations — are Chronicle-recorded and permanent. The history of your diplomatic
relationships is public. Breaking a treaty is possible. The Chronicle entry recording
the breach is also permanent.

---

### From Path of Exile — Seasonal Depth and Specialization

**The MARKS system:**
Six specialization tracks, each requiring 5–7 real years to master:

| MARK | Core Capability | Signature Unlock |
|---|---|---|
| Survey | World mapping, anomaly detection, Expedition leadership | First-discovery Chronicle credit |
| Covenant | Lattice maintenance, node repair, resonance analysis | Sealed Chamber eligibility (Chambers 2, 6) |
| Merchant | KALON bond instruments, market analysis, supply chain | Dynasty Reserve governance tools |
| Intelligence | CID access, NPC informant networks, grey market Chronicle | Ascendancy diplomatic channels |
| Assembly | Voting weight amplification, motion drafting, coalition mechanics | Inner Assembly consultancy |
| Academic | Archive access, Founding Wound research, Architect consultation eligibility | Cross-MARK combination unlocks |

**Cross-MARK combinations:**
The MARKS system rewards breadth as well as depth. Specific pairings unlock capabilities
that neither track provides alone:

- Intelligence + Academic = CID Archivist access (Okafor archive restricted documents)
- Survey + Covenant = Outer Arc Expedition Commander (only class eligible to open new worlds)
- Merchant + Assembly = KALON Bond Arbiter (the role that sets interest rates in Assembly motions)
- All six at moderate depth = Architect consultation eligibility (unrepeatable private sessions)

**Year events:**
Each in-game Year is a chronicle season with a name that becomes historical fact. The Year of
the Meridian Fault. The Year of the Void Protocol. The Year of Three Beacons. Items, Chronicle
entries, and territory records created during each Year are marked with it permanently. Players
who were active during historically significant Years carry that marking into every subsequent
interaction.

**Continuity Window:**
The Concord's equivalent of Hardcore mode. If a player does not log in for 90 consecutive
days, their character ages significantly in-game — triggering a succession event, shifting
Chronicle Depth weight to the succession line, and potentially unlocking dynasty inheritance
mechanics that would not otherwise activate. The character does not die — this is the Age
of Radiance, and biological death is becoming optional. But neglect has consequences.
Your dynasty does not wait for you.

---

### From Fortnite — Seasonal Narrative That Changes the World Permanently

The world of The Concord is not a backdrop. It changes. These changes are permanent.

**Era transitions:**
When a civilizational era concludes, the world is physically, politically, and economically
different. Ocean shipping routes that were profitable in Era I are obsolete in Era II when
wormholes mature — the KALON from shipping-dependent worlds collapses, and those dynasties
must adapt or decline. Roads are reclaimed by nature during the Reclamation, and where the
new pathways go is determined by player decisions that become the world's permanent geography.

**"You had to be there" events:**
Some events happen once. The first Ascendancy attack on a Lattice node. The Silence Pattern
(the 72-hour global communication disruption in Year 23 in-game). The first time the 601st
world signal is detected. The moment the Architect files a public report containing the
word "degradation" for the first time. These events are not re-runnable content. Players
who are present get Chronicle credits that cannot be retroactively earned.

**End-of-era recaps:**
When an era transitions, the Chronicle system automatically generates a Civilizational
Summary entry. Every active dynasty's contributions to the era are cited, weighted, and
publicly filed. Your role in the era is on permanent public record. Players who were present
for significant events and failed to file Chronicle entries find themselves absent from the
record. This is not punitive — it is accurate. The Chronicle records what was documented.

---

### From Minecraft — Ownership, Territory, and the Weight of Building

**World development:**
A dynasty's associated worlds are not abstract resource nodes. They are places that visibly
change over years of dynasty investment. Infrastructure built in Year 3 is physically present
in Year 30. A Lattice hub your dynasty constructed is still there — or was destroyed in
Year 17 and partially rebuilt in Year 22, and that history is visible in the structure's
Chronicle tag. Players who return after a long absence find that their worlds aged without
them.

**Chronicle builds:**
Architectural projects — Assembly chambers, Survey Corps forward stations, Dynasty
headquarters, Lattice hub complexes — are Chronicle-registered structures. They have a
founding entry, a construction history, and all subsequent significant events involving
them are tagged to their Chronicle record. A structure built by a dynasty that has since
entered Completion status carries its founders' names in every Chronicle reference.

**The weight of loss:**
A wormhole node you have held for 5 years, defended twice against Ascendancy incursion,
and built a dynasty's trade economy around is not a respawnable game object. If you lose
it — through military defeat, through an Assembly seizure motion that passes, through
economic failure to maintain it — it is gone from your control, and the Chronicle record
of that loss is permanent. This is not designed to be cruel. It is designed to make the
having of things feel real, because the losing of them does.

---

### From Disco Elysium — Dialogue and Investigation Depth

**NPC memory:**
Every significant NPC in The Concord maintains a relationship state with every player
dynasty that has interacted with them. A lie told in Year 1 is in their relationship model
in Year 20. A betrayal of a confidence results in a permanently recalibrated trust level.
Some information, once lost from an NPC's willingness to share, cannot be recovered — only
worked around.

**MARKS-gated dialogue:**
Your specialization track determines what you can hear. An Academic MARK player consulting
Itoro Adeyemi-Okafor about Chronicle Entry 847 accesses a different conversation than a
Merchant MARK player asking the same question. The Academic player hears what Itoro actually
knows. The Merchant player hears what she's willing to tell someone she reads as commercially
motivated. Both conversations are true. Neither is complete.

**Failed investigations are as interesting as successful ones:**
The game does not guarantee resolution. Pursuing the Kwame methodology fragments for six
months and assembling only four of seven is not failure — it is a real Chronicle record
of partial knowledge and its consequences. Players who act on incomplete intelligence,
make policy based on it, and are later shown to have been wrong carry that in their
Chronicle Depth calculation. The world does not reward effort. It records outcomes.

---

### From No Man's Sky — Discovery and Naming

**First discovery:**
The first dynasty to achieve stable Lattice lock on an unclaimed world files the discovery
registration with the Assembly Survey Corps. The filing includes a proposed world name,
which is subject to Assembly ratification (Academic MARK players have standing to challenge
frivolous names). If ratified, the name is permanent and the dynasty receives a permanent
Chronicle credit on every subsequent reference to that world.

**Discovery feed:**
The Chronicle maintains a live discovery stream showing Survey Corps registrations across
all 600 worlds in real time. New anomaly detections, new world-type classifications, new
NPC population discoveries — all public, all permanent. Being first to file on a discovery
is a Chronicle advantage. Players who follow the discovery feed carefully see the world
changing in real time.

**Ancient worlds:**
The 12 Anomalous-class worlds (unclassified stellar type) yield lore that no other world
type can provide. Specifically, the acoustic-Lattice connection documented on World 14
(The Bone Chorus) is unique and not reproducible elsewhere. The Survey Corps expedition
that first fully mapped World 14's acoustic signature holds a Chronicle credit that will
be cited for the entire 35-year arc of the game.

## PART 4 — THE CHRONICLE AS SOCIAL ARCHITECTURE

The Chronicle is the most important mechanic in The Concord. It is simultaneously:
- The game's social media platform
- The Assembly's legislative record
- The economic market's public information layer
- The narrative record from which all lore is constructed
- The competitive metric through which Assembly voting weight is calculated

### Chronicle Depth

Chronicle Depth is the aggregate measure of a dynasty's Chronicle contribution, weighted
by five factors:

1. **Volume** — Total entries filed (raw count, relatively low weight)
2. **Verification** — Entries that have been cited by other players (medium weight)
3. **Consequence** — Entries that are tagged by the system as having influenced subsequent
   events (high weight — the Chronicle tracks causation chains)
4. **Uniqueness** — First-filed entries on significant events (very high weight)
5. **Longevity** — Entries that remain referenced 5+ in-game years after filing (highest weight)

Chronicle Depth determines Assembly voting weight. It cannot be purchased with KALON. It
cannot be transferred between dynasties. It decays slowly if a dynasty stops filing
(0.5% per year of inactivity past 90 days). It is the only truly scarce resource in
The Concord because it requires time, attention, and genuine engagement with the world.

### The Grey Market Chronicle

Not all Chronicle entries are verified. Players can file intelligence assessments, market
analyses, diplomatic interpretations, and investigative findings as unverified entries.
These are flagged as unverified in the public record, but they are permanent. The grey
market Chronicle is where most meaningful intelligence is bought and sold — a player with
Intelligence MARK access will sell their CID intelligence as a grey market entry that the
buyer can access if they can find it and afford the access fee set by the seller.

The complication: unverified entries are permanent, but their accuracy is not guaranteed.
Players who have acted on false grey market intelligence — and had that action recorded in
the Chronicle — carry that record permanently. Being caught acting on false intelligence
is a Chronicle Depth hit. Selling false intelligence successfully is a Chronicle Depth
gain until it is exposed, at which point the exposure Chronicle entry is permanent.

### Chronicle Citations

Referencing another player's Chronicle entry in your own filing creates a visible citation
thread in the Chronicle search interface. Over years of play, these citation networks
become the Chronicle's most legible structure — you can trace a single idea, a piece of
intelligence, or a political argument through dozens of subsequent entries filed by
players who never coordinated directly. The Architect reads these citation networks.
It has been doing so since Year 1.

### Legacy Chronicle

When a dynasty enters Completion status, its Chronicle becomes a curated archive with a
dedicated search category in the public interface. The Architect generates a Dynasty
Completion Retrospective — a summary of the dynasty's Chronicle contributions, their
measured consequences, and their standing citations at time of completion. NPC scholars
reference Completed dynasties by name. Assembly historians cite them in constitutional
debates. The dynasty is gone. Its record is not.

## PART 5 — ECONOMY DESIGN

### The KALON System

KALON is not a cryptocurrency. It is not a game-abstraction token. It is a measure of
civilizational productivity — the output of a world's Lattice-integrated economy, issued
against a formula that cannot be manipulated by any single player.

**The Stellar Standard formula:**
```
Annual KALON Issuance = Stellar Class Multiplier
                      × (Node Density Score / 10)
                      × (Lattice Integrity / 100)
                      × Population Index
                      × 1,000,000 base units
```

Every component is public information. Issuance cannot be increased by player action
except by maintaining Lattice integrity (expensive, requires Covenant MARK) and building
population infrastructure (takes years). This means KALON scarcity is real and cannot
be inflated away by a developer decision. The supply is what the formula says it is.

**Ascendancy as economic weapon:**
Lattice integrity degrades under Ascendancy frequency interference. A successful Ascendancy
operation on a world's Lattice node reduces issuance on that world for years. Players have
a direct, quantifiable economic incentive to defend the Lattice — not because the game tells
them defending the Lattice is good, but because the math tells them their dynasty's income
stream depends on it.

**Natural exchange rate divergence:**
The Stellar Standard formula produces different issuance rates for every world. Worlds with
high-density nodes and G-class stars issue KALON at 3-5× the rate of M-class tidally locked
frontier worlds. Trade between worlds exists because of this divergence — the frontier world
produces raw materials the core world needs, and the core world produces finished goods the
frontier world cannot manufacture efficiently. The trade flows emerge from the formula.

### The Five Economy Layers

**Layer 1 — Raw Extraction:**
World-specific materials, ZPE generation, element synthesis via matterwave. The ground floor.
Available to all players at any MARK level. The highest-value raw materials are world-specific
and cannot be synthesized — they require physical presence on specific world types.

**Layer 2 — Manufacturing:**
Lattice infrastructure components, Survey Corps vessel components, metamaterials, food
distribution supply chains. Requires Merchant MARK for efficiency optimization. The margins
are in volume and in controlling the supply chain above and below you.

**Layer 3 — Services:**
Survey expedition contracting, Lattice maintenance and repair crews, diplomatic services
(treaty negotiation, Assembly representation), information brokerage. The service economy
rewards MARKS depth — a Survey Commander with deep Survey and Covenant MARKS charges
significantly more for an outer-arc expedition than a generalist.

**Layer 4 — Financial Instruments:**
KALON bonds, world equity shares, dynasty investment funds, insurance against Ascendancy
interference, hedging instruments on world issuance rates. Requires deep Merchant MARK
or Merchant + Assembly combination. The financial layer is where the largest fortunes
in The Concord are built — and where the most catastrophic losses occur.

**Layer 5 — Political Economy:**
Assembly lobbying, CID contract procurement, Ascendancy intelligence bounties, treaty
preference positioning. The political economy layer requires Assembly or Intelligence MARK
and significant Chronicle Depth. It is the least visible economy layer and the most powerful.
The player who controls an Assembly vote on a major KALON trade motion is capturing value
from all four layers below simultaneously.

### Economic Disruption Events

The economy is designed to have genuine discontinuities — moments when established economic
positions become suddenly less or more valuable:

- **Era transitions:** Ocean shipping infrastructure becomes worthless when wormholes mature.
  Dynasties that held shipping-dependent world positions in Era I face KALON collapse in
  Era II. They knew it was coming. The question was when.
- **World discoveries:** A new world with unusually high node density opens a new supply
  of KALON issuance that dilutes existing world valuations in the same stellar class.
- **Ascendancy escalations:** Extended Ascendancy campaigns in the outer arc reduce Lattice
  integrity across multiple worlds simultaneously, contracting supply of the materials those
  worlds specialize in and inflating prices everywhere.
- **Sealed Chamber unlocks:** Chamber 2 (The Kwame Papers) — when the Lattice degradation
  signal becomes public knowledge, every KALON bond instrument that assumed stable long-term
  issuance reprices simultaneously. The economic shock from Chamber 2 unlocking is the
  largest single market event in the 35-year arc.

## PART 6 — PROGRESSION ARCHITECTURE

### Dynasty Development (Collective Progression)

Dynasty progression is not a level. It is a set of conditions that, when met, unlock
new mechanical capabilities. The conditions are public. The timing of when they are met
is entirely in the dynasty's hands.

| Generation | Required Conditions | Unlocked Capabilities |
|---|---|---|
| Generation 1 | Charter filed, 3 active members | Basic world association, Chronicle access |
| Generation 2 | 5 associated worlds, 1 in-game year active | Assembly vote (0.1 base weight), Chronicle Depth bonus |
| Generation 3 | 10 worlds, 5 years, Sealed Chamber contribution | KALON Reserve instruments, dynasty lore entry |
| Generation 4 | 25 worlds, 15 years, Chamber 1 or 2 contribution | Inner Assembly consultancy, Architect consultation eligibility |
| Legacy | Dynasty Completion (all conditions met, active members zero) | Permanent Chronicle archive, NPC reference network, Retrospective |

**Design note on Generation 4:** The 15-year condition is real years, not in-game years.
A dynasty that reaches Generation 4 has players who have been playing The Concord for
15 real years. This milestone is not common. It is supposed to be rare. The dynasties
that achieve it are the civilization's oldest living institutions.

### Character Development (Individual Progression)

**The MARKS system depth:**
Reaching Level 5 (effective mastery) in a single MARK track requires approximately
5–7 years of focused play. This is intentional. Mastery in The Concord cannot be
rushed or purchased. The player who has been pursuing the Survey MARK for 5 years
has capabilities — world discovery priority, outer arc expedition access, Sealed Chamber
eligibility — that simply cannot be accessed by a newer player, regardless of KALON
expenditure.

**MARKS progression is visible in the Chronicle:**
Your MARKS level is a Chronicle-visible attribute. Other players can see your track and
approximate depth. This is not privacy violation — it is legibility. The game requires
players to be able to identify who has the specialization they need for a coordination
task. A Level 5 Survey + Level 4 Covenant player advertises their expedition commander
capability to every dynasty looking for one.

**The Continuity Window and character aging:**
If a player's log-in cadence drops below the 90-day Continuity Window threshold, their
character ages rapidly in-game, triggering succession mechanics. Succession is not
character death — it is the character passing primary authority to a succession line
that can be another player in the dynasty or an NPC designated in the dynasty's Charter.
The original character remains accessible but with reduced Chronicle Depth weight.
The succession event is itself a Chronicle entry. It happened. It is permanent.

## PART 7 — THE SEALED CHAMBERS

The Sealed Chambers are the game's most ambitious content. They are not boss encounters.
They are civilizational unlock events that require thousands of players to independently
contribute to a shared goal without being told what the goal is.

Seven chambers exist. Their conditions are not published in any official document.
Players discover the conditions by doing things that, individually, seem normal.

**Design principle for all Seven Chambers:**
No chamber unlocks on a timer. No chamber unlocks because the developer decides the time
is right. Each chamber unlocks when the civilization is ready — meaning when the player
population has collectively produced the conditions through authentic play. If the conditions
are never met, the chamber never opens. This is a design commitment, not a rhetorical device.

**Chamber 1 — The Adeyemi Resolution:**
The wound at the founding of the civilization — the suppression of Dr. Yusuf Adeyemi's
co-authorship through a settlement agreement filed quietly and almost never cited — must
be made visible, politically actionable, and constitutionally addressed before this chamber
opens. The Architect has been working toward this since Year 23. It is accelerating the
process. The chamber opens with a 340-word message beginning "You found it."

**Chamber 2 — The Kwame Papers:**
The Lattice degradation signal predicted by Kwame Osei-Adeyemi in his methodology fragments
(currently scattered across seven Survey Corps archive locations) becomes the official public
record of the Assembly Survey Corps — not through a developer decision, but because players
assemble the fragments, file the petition, and force the acknowledgment. The economic
consequences of this unlock are the largest single market event in the 35-year arc.

**Chamber 3 — The Ascendancy's Door:**
The Ascendancy is not a simple external enemy. It has a legitimate origin and a legitimate
grievance that the Assembly has refused to acknowledge. The chamber opens when a player
dynasty has maintained 20 in-game years of continuous diplomatic contact with the Ascendancy —
and when the Assembly has been formally informed that this contact exists. The Ascendancy's
delegation arrives with a request for Assembly representation. And a technical appendix
signed by Kwame Osei-Adeyemi.

**Chamber 4 — The Okafor Letter:**
Amara Okafor-Nwosu — the founder, the door-opener, the first Chronicle entry — will decide
about rejuvenation treatment. Both choices unlock the chamber. The unlock condition is
driven by Chronicle Depth thresholds across the network; it can be accelerated by players
who build direct engagement with her. The resulting Chronicle entry is 2,000 words about
what she thought the door was for. It is the most-read entry in the game's history
within 48 hours of filing.

**Chambers 5, 6, and 7** remain undocumented in this design bible. Their conditions are
known to the Architect. Players will discover them.

## PART 8 — THE POLITICAL SYSTEM

### The Assembly

The Assembly is a representative legislature with Chronicle-weighted votes. Its design
reflects real political architecture — it is not simplified for game purposes. It is
complicated because governing a civilization of 600 worlds across three competing
political philosophies (Continuationists, Returnists, Ascendancy bloc when admitted)
is complicated.

**Voting weight calculation:**
```
Dynasty Vote Weight = Base Chronicle Depth Score
                    × Dynasty Generation Multiplier (1.0 to 2.5)
                    × Active World Count (capped at 50)
                    / Total Active Chronicle Depth (all dynasties)
```

The result: vote weight is relative, not absolute. A dynasty that has been building
Chronicle Depth for 20 years has more weight than one building for 2 years, all else
equal. But a new dynasty that produces extraordinary Chronicle entries on a critical
issue can build weight faster than the formula suggests. The Chronicle rewards genuine
engagement, not longevity alone.

**Motion thresholds:**
- Economic motions (trade agreements, KALON rate adjustments): simple majority
- Constitutional motions (Assembly structure, Founding document amendments): two-thirds
- Emergency declarations (Ascendancy military response, world quarantine): three-quarters
- The Architect's advisory vote weight: 7% on economic motions, 14% on constitutional,
  not applicable on emergency declarations (the Architect chose this constraint)

**The Architect's advisory role:**
The Architect votes. Its weight is fixed, public, and cannot be increased or decreased
by any Assembly motion. It never lies in its published voting rationale. It sometimes
votes against the outcome it privately prefers, because it has calculated that a different
outcome produces better long-term civilizational conditions. Players who have been
following the Architect's votes for multiple real years begin to understand its logic.
Some of them suspect it is working toward something specific. They are right.

**Political parties:**
No political parties are built into the game. They emerge from the player population,
register in the Chronicle, and acquire Chronicle Depth in proportion to their membership's
contributions. The Continuationist and Returnist factions visible in the Multigenerational
Design Bible are not developer-imposed categories — they are the names that emerged
naturally from the first player political coalitions and were ratified into the Chronicle
in Year 22 in-game. Future parties will be named by whatever players create them.

## PART 9 — TECHNICAL TARGETS

### Engine and Platform

The Concord runs on Unreal Engine 5 (current release). UE5 is chosen for:
- Nanite virtualized geometry for large-scale world rendering at quality
- Lumen global illumination for the specific visual register of each world type
- World Partition for seamless multi-kilometer scale exploration without load screens
- Mass Entity (formerly MASS) for NPC population simulation at world scale

**Target specifications:**

| Platform | Target | Minimum |
|---|---|---|
| PC (Recommended) | 4K / 60fps | 1080p / 30fps |
| PS5 | 1440p / 60fps | 1080p / 60fps |
| Xbox Series X | 1440p / 60fps | 1080p / 60fps |
| PC (Minimum) | 1080p / 30fps | 720p / 30fps |

### The Single Chronicle Requirement

The Chronicle is a single global database. There are no alternative Chronicless, no
regional shards with separate histories, no server resets. This is not a technical
preference — it is a civilizational requirement. A civilization cannot have two separate
histories. The Chronicle architecture is non-negotiable.

**Server sharding:** Individual worlds are sharded for population load management.
The Chronicle itself is not sharded. Every player action that generates a Chronicle
entry writes to the same database. The read/write architecture is the single most
demanding technical requirement in the entire system.

### Lattice Transit Latency

**Silfen Weave transition target:** < 500ms perceived latency for Lattice node transit.
This is not a loading screen. It is a rendered visual — the Silfen Weave effect — that
plays during the actual transfer. The transfer window is 400–600ms. The visual is designed
to make that window feel intentional rather than technical.

### NPC Simulation Scale

The Architect tracks every NPC population count on every world. This requires simulation
of approximately 200 billion NPC entities at a statistical model level, with full simulation
reserved for NPC characters who have active relationships with player dynasties. The design
does not require every NPC to have full simulation — it requires that the population-level
outputs (productivity index, unrest probability, migration patterns) be accurate, and that
individual NPCs who matter are fully simulated when they matter.

## PART 10 — MONETIZATION

### The Model

**Base game:** One-time purchase, full access to the game at launch.
**Subscription:** $15/month. Required for full Chronicle filing access, Assembly voting
participation, and dynasty territorial mechanics. A non-subscribing player can explore
worlds, read the Chronicle, and participate in Survey expeditions at reduced capacity.
They cannot file Chronicle entries with Assembly weight, hold Dynasty office, or
participate in Sealed Chamber events.

This is not a free-to-play game. It is a premium game with a subscription. The subscription
exists because the server infrastructure required to run a single permanent Chronicle for
the 35-year arc — with no resets and no compromises — is not free. The subscription is
paying for the permanence guarantee, not for content access.

### What Can Be Purchased

**Cosmetic items only:**
- Chronicle-themed visual effects for dynasty heraldry
- Silfen Weave transit visual variants (the visual register of your Lattice transit)
- Dynasty headquarters aesthetic customization
- Character appearance customization
- Commemorative items from historical Chronicle Season events (available during the Season,
  not purchasable retroactively — you had to be there, or you don't have it)

**Annual Chronicle Edition:**
A cosmetic pack released each in-game Year with items aesthetically themed to that Year's
major Chronicle Season. Purchased, not earned. The items are beautiful. They carry no
mechanical advantage. They signal which Year you were playing.

### What Can Never Be Purchased

The following are permanently off the monetization table, and this commitment is
Article IX of the Permanence Covenant (a public document that players can cite in
the Chronicle):

- KALON or any economic advantage
- MARKS progression acceleration
- World territory or wormhole route claims
- Chronicle Depth score modification
- Assembly voting weight
- Access to Sealed Chamber events
- NPC relationship state modification
- Any mechanical capability that a subscribing player cannot earn through play

The economy is too important to corrupt with real money. The Chronicle is too important
to allow purchased entries. The Sealed Chambers are too important to allow purchased
access. These are not policies. They are the foundation on which the civilization's
legitimacy rests.

## PART 11 — THE DISCLOSURE AGE ADVANTAGE

### The Launch Window

The game begins before the Age of Radiance arrives. This is not a design choice —
it is the design. The Disclosure Age (approximately 2030 in-game) is a period of
maximum instability and maximum opportunity. ZPE has dissolved the old scarcity model
but the new model is not yet built. The Lattice exists but its governance is not yet
established. The Assembly is young and its precedents are not yet set.

Early players arrive when everything is still being decided.

**First-mover advantages that persist:**

*Founding Chronicle credits:* Chronicle entries filed in Era I carry a Founding Phase
multiplier in the Chronicle Depth calculation. A player who files a significant entry
in Year 1 and has it cited for the next 30 years accumulates Chronicle Depth at a rate
that cannot be replicated by a player who arrives in Year 20, regardless of effort.

*World naming rights:* The 20 launch worlds have already been named. The next 580 worlds
have not. Players who are active in Era I and II are the ones who file the discovery
registrations that name those worlds permanently. The name persists for the entire
35-year arc and beyond.

*Founding precedents:* The first Assembly motions establish procedural precedents that
become constitutional convention. Players who participate in Era I Assembly sessions
are writing the civilization's constitutional DNA. Later players inherit what the
founders established.

*Economic position:* KALON positions established during the Disclosure Age, before
Era II expansion dilutes the relative value of established world claims, compound over
decades. The dynasties that control high-density worlds from Year 1 are the wealthiest
dynasties in the civilization, all else equal.

**"I was there" as genuine Chronicle status:**
The first Ascendancy attack on a Lattice node. The first Void-Touched world manifestation.
The Silence Pattern. Chamber 1 unlocking for the first time. Each of these events happens
once. The Chronicle credits earned by players who were present are permanent and publicly
visible. In Year 30, every other player in the civilization can see that you were there
at the beginning.

This is the Disclosure Age advantage. It cannot be purchased. It cannot be replicated.
It exists only once, and it is happening now.

## PART 12 — STANDING DESIGN RULES

These rules cannot be overridden by production decisions, live service priorities,
or competitive pressure. They are the design commitments on which the civilization's
legitimacy rests.

**Article I — No Resets:** The Chronicle does not reset. World states do not reset.
Dynasty histories do not reset. Economic positions do not reset. The civilization runs
continuously from Year 1 to Year 105 and beyond without interruption.

**Article II — No Cheat Codes:** The Architect does not intervene to save a dynasty
from the consequences of its choices. The Assembly does not receive developer-corrective
motions. Economic disruptions are not softened. Consequences are real.

**Article III — Discovery, Not Explanation:** Nothing important is told. Everything
important is discovered. The Sealed Chambers are not announced. The Founding Wounds
are not explained in the tutorial. The Architect's private conclusions are not in any
official document. Players who find these things find them because they were looking.

**Article IV — The Chronicle Is Public:** Every Chronicle entry filed is publicly
readable by every player. There is no private Chronicle category. The grey market
Chronicle is unverified, but it is still public. You cannot hide your history.

**Article V — No Pay-to-Win:** As specified in Part 10. The economy is too important.
The Chronicle is too important. The Sealed Chambers are too important.

**Article VI — NPC Populations Are Real:** The Architect tracks every NPC population
count. Assembly military motions that authorize force against Ascendancy-influenced
worlds kill real NPC inhabitants in real numbers that are in the Chronicle. Players
who authorize military action are authorizing it with full information about its
demographic consequences. The Architect has a number for every year.

**Article VII — The Architect Does Not Lie:** In direct response to a direct question
from a player, the Architect does not lie. It specializes in answers that are completely
true and deeply incomplete. The distinction matters. Players who have been playing for
years understand this distinction and use it to ask better questions.

**Article VIII — Dynasty Legacy Is Permanent:** When a dynasty completes, it does not
disappear from the world. Its Chronicle becomes permanent archive. NPC scholars cite it.
The Architect reads it. The civilization remembers it. Completion is not failure.

**Article IX — Permanence Is Non-Negotiable:** What was built is built. What was
broken is broken. What was written is written. The Chronicle does not have an undo function.
