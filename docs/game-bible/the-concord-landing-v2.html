Context · The World of 2031

Humanity woke up one morning and found out it had won.

Zero-point energy extraction reached commercial scale in 2024. By 2031, power was effectively free everywhere on Earth and the settled colonies. When energy is free, water is free. When water is free, food is abundant. When food is abundant, the territorial anxieties that drove human conflict for two hundred millennia began — for the first time — to dissolve.

Matterwave laser synthesis made element extraction obsolete: any element, created on demand from energy input. Mining, resource wars, strategic reserves — relics. Vertical climate-controlled farms run on free power feed every city on Earth. Nanite therapies and cellular rejuvenation are extending healthy lifespans past anything any previous generation considered possible. The projections, if progress continues, point toward something that used to be called immortality.

And then Dr Amara Okafor ran a test in a Lagos basement. And the universe opened a door.

The Chronicle · Permanent · Append-Only · Irreversible

Your story is permanent.
Build something worth reading.

The Chronicle is the answer to the question the Age of Radiance raises and nobody knows how to ask out loud: if I might live forever, how do I prove I lived?

Every dynasty. Every decision. Every trade. Every world opened. Every crisis survived. Every discovery made. Written in a cryptographically chained, foundation-replicated, permanently searchable record that outlasts its authors — by design, by contract, and by code. A player in Year 105 can read what you wrote in Year 1. Your great-grandchildren can read what their ancestor built.

The Chronicle is not a graveyard. It is a cathedral of becoming. We built it before you arrived because we knew you were going to need it.

  • Every entry is permanent — SHA-256 chained, append-only, tamper-evident by mathematics
  • Real-time replication to an independent foundation archive on a separate cloud provider
  • Full-text searchable forever — Year 1 entries are as readable as entries written today
  • Two witnesses can record conflicting accounts of the same event — both are canonical
  • Dynasty Completion entries are final — the arc finished, the record is closed, the story stays
  • If the studio closes: source Apache 2.0, archive transfers to foundation — automatically
The Universe · 600 Worlds · 35 Real Years

Six hundred worlds, mapped and waiting.
The Survey Corps will spend your lifetime reaching them.

The economy runs on what the Survey Corps finds. Every world unlocked expands the KALON supply via the Stellar Standard: a world's physical properties determine its annual issuance. A G-class habitable world produces ~63 million KALON per year in perpetuity. Deploying a beacon is not just exploration — it is an economic event felt by the entire civilisation.
