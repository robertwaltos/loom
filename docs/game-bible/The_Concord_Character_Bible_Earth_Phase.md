# THE CONCORD — CHARACTER BIBLE
## Earth Phase · Pre-Year 0 Characters
## Project Threshold · The Disclosure · The Founding Departure

---

*These characters predate the Concord's founding. They are the people who built the gate, controlled it, lost control of it, walked through it, or were left behind. By Year 105 they are dead, mythologised, classified, or — in rare cases — still alive in rejuvenation, carrying knowledge no institution has been able to pry from them.*

*In-game, Earth Phase characters exist primarily as Chronicle archive figures. Their records, where not classified, are the foundation layer of the Chronicle — the entries that explain why the Concord was built the way it was. Players who excavate deep enough find that every major Concord institutional flaw traces back to a decision made by someone in this document.*

*Dating convention: Earth Phase events are given as approximate real-world years (2040s–2060s). In-universe they are referenced as "pre-Year 0" or "Founding Era."*

### E-001 · DR. YEWANDE ADISA-REINHOLT
**Program Role:** Threshold Program — Chief Theoretical Physicist, Gate Architecture Division
**Earth-era approximate age:** 44 at disclosure (~2051)
**In-Concord status:** Deceased Year 31 — founding-generation figure, fourth person to walk through the gate

**Wound:** Spent eleven years building a theoretical framework for stable wormhole aperture generation — and spent those same eleven years in a DoD black program that legally prevented her from publishing any of it. Her entire intellectual life's work entered the world classified. When disclosure came, her papers were released — but the classified origin meant they were released as government documents, not academic publications. Her name appears in the acknowledgements of twelve subsequent papers by other physicists who built on her work. She is not listed as a primary author on any of them.

**Limitation:** Could not re-establish academic priority for work the government owned — the IP structure of the Threshold Program assigned all derivative intellectual property to DoD. The legal challenge died with her.

**Competence:** The foundational mathematics of stable aperture generation — specifically the recursive harmonic correction model that prevents aperture collapse under gravitational variance. Every gate ever built uses her correction model. The equation is named the Reinholt Constant in post-disclosure physics. The attribution fight about whether it should be the Adisa-Reinholt Constant continued for twenty years after her death.

**Question:** Whether she would have chosen differently — whether the eleven classified years were the price of getting it built, or whether the same work could have been done in the open and arrived at the same place.

**Secret:** In Year 3, she filed a private Chronicle entry that is the most technically detailed account of the gate's design process ever recorded. She filed it under a restriction classification she invented herself — one that no Chronicle protocol supported. The entry has been sitting in the archive for 28 years in a classification the Chronicle system doesn't know how to process. It has never been reviewed.

**Visual Prompts:**

> **Gemini:** Full-body portrait of a 44-year-old woman of Nigerian-Nordic heritage. No institutional uniform — she was classified as a DoD contractor and wore civilian clothes her entire tenure. Natural black hair worn in a large structured afro. Eyes that spent eleven years building something they couldn't publish. In a sparse government research facility — the gate aperture visible through reinforced glass behind her, the first stable full-scale gate in the history of the human species. She is looking at it, not at the camera. Photorealistic. Threshold Program facility harsh fluorescent and monitor glow.

> **Grok:** Ultra-realistic portrait. 44-year-old woman, Nigerian-Nordic synthesis. DoD contractor civilian wear. Structured afro. She built the mathematical foundation every gate in the Concord uses. Her work was classified for eleven years. When it was released she wasn't listed as a primary author on any of the derivative papers. She is looking at the gate. She built it. Threshold Program facility lighting.

### E-002 · COLONEL JAMES OSEI-MARCHETTI (RET.)
**Program Role:** Threshold Program — Military Liaison and Security Director, Years 1–9
**Earth-era approximate age:** 52 at disclosure (~2051)
**In-Concord status:** Deceased Year 18 — did not emigrate; died on Earth seven years after disclosure

**Wound:** Spent nine years as the person responsible for keeping Project Threshold secret. Built the cover operation — a classified atmospheric research program — that successfully concealed the Threshold Program from congressional oversight, foreign intelligence, and the scientific community. Was good at it. When the cover story collapsed, the cover operation's construction — including the deliberate misdirection of three congressional intelligence briefings — became the primary basis for the Senate investigation. He was the most senior person who could be held responsible without implicating the principals who had authorised everything he did.

**Limitation:** Could not reveal the authorisation chain without violating classification agreements he had signed — and which the principals had not yet declassified. He was prosecuted on the basis of actions that were, in his understanding, authorised. He did not reveal the authorisation chain. He was the only person who went to prison.

**Competence:** Operational security architecture for long-duration covert programs — the specific discipline of maintaining a cover story across multiple administrations, budget cycles, and personnel rotations without a single significant breach for nine years.

**Question:** Whether the nine years of successful concealment were a service to a program that genuinely needed protection, or whether he was the instrument of a decision made above him that he executed with a professionalism that insulated the decision-makers from consequence.

**Secret:** The three congressional briefings he misdirected were authorised in writing by a Deputy Secretary of Defense whose name was never released in the Senate investigation — classified on national security grounds. The authorisation documents exist. They are still classified. He carried this knowledge to his grave without disclosing it.

Eyes that built a nine-year deception that worked perfectly and then became the evidence against him. Standing in a sparse private space — the Senate investigation transcripts on a table behind him. Not looking at them. Photorealistic. Post-disclosure civilian lighting — grey, reduced.

### E-003 · DR. EMEKA OKAFOR-VASQUEZ
**Program Role:** Threshold Program — Aperture Stability Engineering Lead
**Earth-era approximate age:** 38 at disclosure (~2051)
**In-Concord status:** Active — third person to walk through the gate; currently 119 years old (second rejuvenation); location undisclosed

**Wound:** Was the engineer responsible for the gate's physical stability systems — not the theory, but the hardware that turned theory into a door you could walk through. In the nine years before the first successful transit, presided over four catastrophic aperture failures. Two of the failures killed test subjects. He was present for both. The program classified the deaths as training accidents under the cover operation's fiction. He signed the incident reports. He signed them correctly under the classification framework he worked within. He has been living with the signatures for eighty years.

**Limitation:** The incident reports are classified at the highest level — they may never be released.

**Competence:** Aperture stability hardware engineering at the threshold of the physically possible — the specific work of building something that theory says should work but that kept killing people until the thirty-seventh iteration.

**Question:** Whether the two deaths were the inevitable cost of building something genuinely new, or whether there was a point in the iteration sequence where a different decision could have changed the outcome — and whether he has been avoiding that question for eighty years.

**Secret:** The Architect knows about the deaths. In Year 3 it filed a private entry in its own operational log referencing the incident reports by classification number. It has never disclosed this to any human institution. He knows the Architect knows. They have an unspoken arrangement that predates the Concord's existence.

### E-004 · DR. PRIYA SUNDARAM-CHEN
**Program Role:** Threshold Program — Xenophysics Division Lead (Years 4–11)
**Earth-era approximate age:** 36 at disclosure (~2051)
**In-Concord status:** Deceased Year 62 — founding generation; Admiral Yara Sundaram-Chen (character 009) is her granddaughter

**Wound:** Was the person who first formally asked, within the program, what was on the other side of the gate. What came through when it was opened (besides the test subjects who went and came back) had not been studied. She built the xenophysics framework from nothing, alone, in three years, in a classified program that had no budget line for her question because her question hadn't existed before she asked it.

**Limitation:** The xenophysics data she collected pre-disclosure is almost entirely classified — the program's scientific yield from the other side of the gate before Year 0 has never been released.

**Competence:** First-contact scientific methodology — specifically the design of observation protocols for environments that have no prior study, no baseline data, and no established measurement framework. The Survey Corps' entire approach methodology descends directly from her pre-Year 0 xenophysics protocols.

**Question:** What was in the pre-disclosure xenophysics data — what she found on the other side of the gate in the years before anyone else was allowed to know the gate existed.

**Secret:** In Year 2, during an early probe transit, her instruments recorded what she classified internally as "resonance response" — a signal from the destination environment that appeared to react to the probe's presence. She classified it as instrument artifact. She never stopped thinking about it. Her granddaughter Admiral Yara Sundaram-Chen commands the Survey Corps. The Admiral has never been told what her grandmother found on the other side of the first gate.

### E-005 · DR. KOLADE OSEI-LINDQVIST
**Program Role:** Threshold Program — Nuclear Physics Division; whistleblower
**Earth-era approximate age:** 41 at disclosure (~2051)
**In-Concord status:** Deceased Year 44 — the primary leak source; the person who ended the cover story

**Wound:** Joined the Threshold Program in Year 6 as a nuclear physics specialist — specifically to study why the program's subsurface test detonations were yielding 2.5 times their theoretical energy output. Spent two years modelling the anomaly. Found the answer: the gate apertures, when open, were acting as partial energy conduits between spatial contexts — and the test detonations near active apertures were drawing energy from the other side. The math was unambiguous. The program classified the findings immediately.

**Limitation:** He was under classification agreements. He had signed. He spent eight months trying to find a legal path to disclosure. Every path closed.

**Competence:** The specific nuclear physics of aperture-adjacent energy anomalies — understanding why the test yields were anomalous in a way that proved the apertures were real without describing the apertures directly.

**Question:** Whether the disclosure was worth what it cost him — and whether he would choose it again knowing that the disclosure produced chaos, that he was prosecuted, that he spent three years in prison, and that by the time he was released the Concord was already half-built and nobody needed him anymore.

**Secret:** The paper he submitted to three physics journals simultaneously — the act that broke the cover story — contained one deliberate anomaly. A single data point he knew was incorrectly sourced. He included it so that the program would have grounds to challenge the paper's validity, buying time for other physicists to independently verify the core findings before the challenge could succeed. It worked exactly as he planned. Nobody has ever noticed the deliberate anomaly. It is still in the published paper.

Academic casual clothes. Natural black hair close-cropped. Eyes that spent eight months looking for a legal path and found a different one. Seated at a personal workstation — the paper on one screen, three journal submission confirmations on another. This is the moment after he pressed send. Photorealistic. ### E-006 · LIEUTENANT COMMANDER ADAEZE OSEI-BRANDT (USN)
**Program Role:** Threshold Program — First Transit Officer; first human to walk through the gate
**Earth-era approximate age:** 29 at first transit (~2047); 33 at disclosure (~2051)
**In-Concord status:** Deceased Year 71 — first human to cross a wormhole gate; founding generation

**Wound:** Was selected for the first transit because she had the right physiological profile, the right psychological assessment scores, and because she volunteered. Walked through the gate alone on a Thursday morning in 2047 and came back forty-three seconds later with a soil sample and a headache. The program classified the transit immediately. She was debriefed for eighteen hours. She was not permitted to tell anyone what she had done — not her family, not her colleagues, not her therapist. She spent four years knowing she was the first human in history to step on another world and not being able to tell anyone.

**Limitation:** Classification. Four years of classification.

**Competence:** Transit physiological endurance — specifically the capacity to remain functionally coherent during aperture transit, which early transits produced severe disorientation in most test subjects. She reported mild headache. Her neurological response data became the baseline for transit clearance standards.

**Question:** Whether the four years of silence were a wound or something she made her peace with — and whether the fact that "first human through a wormhole" is her permanent historical identity has ever felt like the right description for what those forty-three seconds actually were.

**Secret:** On the other side of the gate, alone, for forty-three seconds, she saw something. Not a landscape. Not a phenomenon. A quality of the light that she described in her classified debrief as "like being recognised." She was 29 years old and she used the word "recognised." The debrief transcript is classified. She filed a personal Chronicle entry in Year 1 describing those forty-three seconds. It is the first personal Chronicle entry in the archive. Its classification is: OPEN.
