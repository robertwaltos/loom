Articles I – IV

The Four Pillars of Permanence

The Permanence Covenant has four articles. Each is legally binding, technically enforced, and designed so that no single party — including the developers — can unilaterally violate it.

I
The World's History Is Permanent
Nothing that has happened in The Concord will ever be erased, retconned, or reset. A world founded in Year Zero remains founded in Year Zero. A dynasty that defended a Lattice gate remains in the Remembrance as its defenders. The battle of Sylvara happened. The fall of Nocturn happened. History does not reset because it is inconvenient. There are no server wipes. There is no rollback. The Concord has no undo.
II
Dynasty NFTs Are Permanent
Every significant achievement — First Contact, World Founding, Gate Defence, Ascendancy Survival — is minted as a Dynasty NFT on the Concord Chain. If the game ever shuts down, a migration protocol activates automatically, moving all Dynasty NFTs to a public Ethereum contract where they remain as historical artefacts in perpetuity. No action required from the player. The contract is already deployed. It waits, as the Concord waited for you.
III
The Remembrance Is Open to All
The full historical record of The Concord — every settled world, every founding declaration, every recorded death, every dynasty lineage, every Covenant violation — is publicly readable without a subscription, without an account, without any barrier whatsoever. You do not need to play The Concord to read its history. The Remembrance is not a feature. It is humanity's record of its second civilisation.
IV
The Source Code Returns to the Commons
If The Concord ever fully shuts down — all servers offline, all development ceased — the complete engine source code is released to the community under an open licence within 30 days. The community may run the Concord on its own infrastructure. The world does not die because the company does. This commitment is not contingent on commercial outcome. It is the condition of building this game at all.
"You are not renting a world. You are not leasing a legacy. What you build in The Concord belongs to you — and to history — for as long as there are humans to remember it."
— The Concord Founding Statement, Year Zero
Articles V – VI

The Doctrine of Succession

The Concord exists in real time. Players live, play, pause, and return. The world continues whether they are present or not. The Succession Doctrine governs what happens to a dynasty during its player's absence — and how the game honours those who choose not to return.

⏳
Days 1–89: The Regent
When a player is absent, the Sentinel Intelligence activates as Regent — managing the dynasty's affairs conservatively, maintaining its world, honouring its existing treaties. The dynasty continues. It does not flourish. It waits for its sovereign.
📜
Day 90: The Dormancy Threshold
At 90 days without account activity, the player's character is formally declared Dormant in the Remembrance. The dynasty passes into the Regent's full stewardship. Other players may petition for diplomatic relations, but not yet for succession.
🏛️
Legacy Dynasties
Extended absence transforms the dynasty into a Legacy Dynasty — a fully AI-governed NPC family carrying the culture, history, and founding philosophy of its player forward. Other players interact with it, trade with it, form alliances. It is part of the world.
⚖️
Reclamation Rights
After 2 real years of absence, a world enters Reclamation status. Active players may petition the Assembly to be recognised as successor. The original player, if they return, may contest. Reclamation outcomes are recorded permanently. The Remembrance does not judge — it remembers.
On the Nature of Digital Mortality

The Concord does not pretend that absence is neutral. In a civilisation where time moves forward and history accumulates, choosing not to participate has consequences — as it does in every civilisation that has ever existed. The Digital Mortality Protocol codifies this truth with precision and without cruelty.

When a player's account lapses beyond 90 days without payment or activity, the following sequence activates:

  • 01 The player's active character is formally recorded as Deceased in the Remembrance — their name, their dynasty's history, their last known location and action, all committed to the permanent record with a date of departure.
  • 02 All personal liquid assets — CONCORDIUM holdings, undeployed credits, personal inventory items — are dissolved into the game's Universal Prosperity Allocation pool, distributed as a fractional increase across all active citizens. The wealth of the departed becomes the sustenance of the living.
  • 03 World Sovereignty, if held, passes to the established Regent under Legacy Dynasty protocol. The dynasty continues. The sovereign line continues. Only the player is gone.
  • 04 The player's Dynasty NFTs remain in their wallet — permanent, untouched, theirs forever. Digital death does not erase legacy. The Remembrance keeps the record regardless of account status.
  • 05 If the player returns and creates a new account, they begin again. A new character. A new family. A new beginning. The old dynasty exists in the Remembrance as history — legible, honourable, complete. It cannot be reclaimed. It was its own story. This one is new.

Legal Provisions — Terms of Service Addendum

Formal TOS Language

The following clauses are incorporated into The Concord's Terms of Service as binding provisions. They are reproduced here for public transparency.

THE CONCORD — TERMS OF SERVICE — PERMANENCE PROVISIONS (SCHEDULE C)
§ C.1 — WORLD HISTORY IMMUTABILITY
The operator warrants that no game event, player action, or world history entry confirmed and recorded in the Remembrance shall be retroactively altered, deleted, or recontextualised by any party, including the operator, without the affirmative consent of a supermajority (67%) of the Player Council. Operator may not unilaterally rewrite confirmed history. Emergency corrections for technical error are permitted within 48 hours of recording only, subject to public disclosure.
§ C.2 — DYNASTY NFT MIGRATION GUARANTEE
In the event of permanent service termination, operator shall execute the NFT Migration Protocol within 30 calendar days, transferring all registered Dynasty NFTs to the public Ethereum migration contract at address [0x — to be published at launch]. This obligation survives corporate dissolution and is secured by a migration bond held in independent escrow. Failure to migrate triggers automatic bond execution.
§ C.3 — REMEMBRANCE PUBLIC ACCESS
The Remembrance API shall remain publicly accessible without authentication or fee for a minimum of 10 years following any service termination, funded by the Remembrance Preservation Reserve (minimum 2% of gross revenue annually). Access includes full read access to all historical records, dynasty lineages, world histories, and Covenant violation logs.
§ C.4 — SOURCE CODE RELEASE OBLIGATION
Upon permanent service termination, operator shall release the complete engine source code under the Apache 2.0 open-source licence within 30 calendar days. Release shall include sufficient documentation for a technically competent third party to operate a functional instance of The Concord. This obligation is irrevocable.
§ C.5 — DIGITAL MORTALITY PROTOCOL (ACCOUNT LAPSE)
Accounts with no payment activity for 90 consecutive calendar days shall be processed under the Digital Mortality Protocol: (a) the active character record shall be updated to Deceased status in the Remembrance with the lapse date recorded; (b) all liquid in-game assets (CONCORDIUM, undeployed credits, non-NFT inventory) shall be dissolved into the UPA distribution pool; (c) World Sovereignty, if held, transfers to Legacy Dynasty Regent status; (d) Dynasty NFTs are not affected and remain in the player's registered wallet; (e) upon account reactivation after lapse, the player must create a new character and dynasty. Prior dynasty records remain in the Remembrance as historical legacy but are not recoverable to active play. Players wishing to preserve active status during temporary absence may activate the Hibernation Protocol (fee: standard monthly rate, no gameplay access, Regent maintains dynasty) for periods up to 24 months.
§ C.6 — PLAYER COUNCIL OVERSIGHT
A Player Council of 12 elected representatives holds co-signing authority over the Dynasty NFT migration contract and the Remembrance archive keys. Operator cannot unilaterally execute migration or archive deletion without Player Council co-signature. Player Council elections are held annually. Voting is open to all players with accounts in good standing for 6+ months.
