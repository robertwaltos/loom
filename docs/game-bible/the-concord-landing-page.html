The Universe · The Lattice · Year Zero

600 worlds. One Lattice. Thirty-five years to reach them all.

Dr Amara Okafor's discovery was not invention — it was observation. Micro-wormholes exist and collapse billions of times per second in every cubic millimetre of space. She learned to hold one open long enough to walk through. What followed was not a technology. It was a door.

The Concord is a single-shard persistent world of 600 planets. At launch, 20 are reachable. The remaining 580 are mapped, confirmed Lattice-compatible, and waiting. The Survey Corps will spend the next 35 real years — your years, your life — unlocking them one by one.

The Permanence Covenant · Nine Articles

Not a terms of service. A contract with teeth.

The Permanence Covenant is deployed on Ethereum L2. It is legally binding. A pre-funded escrow executes automatically on studio closure. We cannot revoke it. We cannot amend it without the Assembly. We cannot pretend it doesn't exist.

  • Your dynasty's history exists in the Remembrance forever. No account closure deletes it.
  • KALON cannot be purchased with real money. Ever. By anyone.
  • The MARKS system lives on Ethereum L2. It does not belong to us.
  • The Remembrance is replicated to an independent foundation archive in real time.
  • If the studio closes, source code is Apache 2.0 within 30 days — automatically.
  • No countdown timers on decisions. No timed exclusives. No FOMO mechanics. Ever.
  • No server wipe. No server merge. No manufactured reset. Not in Year 1, not in Year 35.
  • Gameplay cannot close your account. Only violation of our terms can — and only you.
  • The developer does not have a cheat code. We cannot give ourselves KALON, MARKS, or votes.

Article Nine is the one that matters most. A developer with invisible access to the economy is not a fair arbiter of the economy. So we built a system that makes our own cheating mathematically impossible and publicly verifiable. Every KALON transaction is auditable. The open Economy API makes the developer's thumb on the scale structurally impossible — and provably so.

"The first MMO economy where 'the developer can't cheat'
is not a promise. It is a mathematical fact."

Dynasty · Mortality · Permanence

Your dynasty can fall. You cannot be deleted.

Dynasty Collapse is not death. It is what dynasties do. They fall and rise. A dynasty that has collapsed three times and rebuilt has a Remembrance depth that a comfortable dynasty cannot match.

Three States. Only One Is Permanent.

  • Dynasty Collapse — Recoverable: Lose sovereignty, KALON, political standing. Your history, MARKS, and earned lives remain. Collapse is not failure — it is the price of ambition.
  • Dynasty Extinction — Dynasty ends, account survives: Your dynasty is recorded Deceased in the Remembrance. You begin again, from scratch, on a new world. The Founding Mark if held is the only thing that crosses the divide.
  • Account Closure — ToS violation only: Gameplay cannot close your account. Not resource exhaustion, not permanent defeat, not developer decision. Only a breach of our terms — and only yours.
3 Earned Lives

Every dynasty starts with 3 lives. Cap of 7. Earned through civic contribution, Remembrance depth, survival, and Assembly recognition. A life consumed returns you to pre-collapse state exactly.

90 Day One Protection

No dynasty can be forced to Extinction by player action in its first 90 in-game days. Collapse is still real. But no predator can destroy a new dynasty before it has found its feet.

∞ In Abeyance

When a player's real-world death is documented, their dynasty enters In Abeyance. Heraldic status. A 2-year heir window. The Architect writes a permanent note from the full Remembrance. The game remembers your real-world player. Always.

Mental Health · Anti-Predatory Design

Every mechanic is asked one question: does this deepen investment or exploit it?

Games are one of the primary coping mechanisms for a significant portion of players — not pathologically, but genuinely. A person who is isolated, grieving, or struggling finds real community and real accomplishment in game worlds. Every current game exploits this without acknowledging it.

We built The Concord differently. Every mechanic starts with one question: does this make the Remembrance richer or poorer? Does this deepen investment or exploit it?

  • Countdown timers on any decision, offer, or choice
  • Limited-time events requiring continuous presence
  • Social comparison features designed to create inadequacy
  • Re-engagement notifications designed to pull you back when you've stopped
  • Daily login bonuses or streak mechanics of any kind
  • FOMO mechanics, timed exclusives, or artificial scarcity tied to attention
