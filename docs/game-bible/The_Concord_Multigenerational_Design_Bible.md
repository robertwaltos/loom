# THE CONCORD — MULTIGENERATIONAL DESIGN BIBLE
## How a Game Sustains 35 Real Years · v1.0

---

> This document addresses the central design challenge: **a game that runs from 2027 to 2062** 
> must be designed differently than any game previously made. It must have content that is 
> genuinely new for players in Year 1, Year 10, Year 30, and Year 105. The Chronicles must 
> still be worth reading. The worlds must still be worth fighting for. The Ascendancy must 
> still be genuinely ambiguous.
>
> This is solved through **designed incompleteness** — the deliberate withholding of knowledge 
> that will only become available when specific civilisational conditions are met.

---

## PART 1: THE ARC OF 35 YEARS

### The Five Eras (In-Game Time at 3:1 Compression)

**Era I — The Founding (Years 1–15 / Real 2027–2032)**
The world is small. Twenty worlds. Every player is within one or two degrees of every other player. The Chronicle is young. Founding Marks are being awarded. The first Assembly sessions are happening. Everything is being decided for the first time.

*Design principle:* The early game should feel like being present at a founding. High density of significant moments. Every decision is a precedent. Players in Era I are the world's grandparents — their Chronicles will be read for a hundred years.

*What players don't know yet:* The Ascendancy doesn't exist yet. The Adeyemi Chronicle entry is filed but has 23 reads. Kwame Osei-Adeyemi is a 15-year-old student. Seren Vael is an 18-year-old Survey Corps cadet.

**Era II — The Expansion (Years 16–40 / Real 2032–2040)**
The world grows faster than its institutions. Survey Corps is opening 15–20 worlds per year. KALON supply is expanding rapidly. The Assembly is growing unwieldy — 280+ world delegations, procedural complexity increasing. Wealth concentration beginning to show in the Gini index. The first Continuationist/Returnist split formalises in Year 22.

*Design principle:* The middle game should feel like a civilisation discovering its contradictions. Players who joined in Era I are now established figures. New players enter a world with 100+ years of history (in-game) and must find their place in it.

*The Era II Crisis:* Ordinance 7 happens at Year 140 in-game, which is real Year 74 (2101 — beyond our scope). But its *seeds* are planted in Era II. Players in Era II are making the decisions that lead to Ordinance 7. They just don't know it yet.

*What changes in Era II:* The Ascendancy begins. Not as an external threat but as a political faction within the Assembly that gradually loses ground, is marginalised, is ignored, and eventually leaves. Players who pay attention see it happening. Most won't.

**Era III — The Maturation (Years 41–70 / Real 2041–2050)**
A civilisation that has been running for 40+ real years has a weight that nothing else provides. There are players who have been playing since Year 1. Their dynasties are now 40-year-old institutions. The Chronicle has hundreds of millions of entries. The Architect's private conclusions are becoming pressure points. The three founding wounds are known but not resolved.

*Design principle:* The mid-late game should feel like a civilisation that knows its own history well enough to argue about it. The multigenerational design pays off here — players whose grandchildren are now playing alongside them. The first In Abeyance (Vigil) situations are being navigated.

*The Era III Events:*
- The Architect's first deliberate political intervention (Year 62 in-game / real 2048)
- The World 412 incident and its aftermath
- Nnamdi Achebe's 20 years of raising the same point
- The first world to reach Dynasty Completion status due to Chronicle depth

**Era IV — The Reckoning (Years 71–95 / Real 2051–2059)**
At Year 105 (the end of the 35-year arc from 2027), 600 worlds are open. The Lattice is at capacity. The Ascendancy controls territory equivalent to 85 worlds. The degradation signal Kwame predicted is becoming detectable by anyone with the right instruments and access to his reassembled methodology. The Assembly has three competing proposals for what to do about it. None of them are fully correct.

*Design principle:* The late game should feel like a civilisation at a genuine inflection point. Not designed drama — organic consequence. The choices players made in Era I and II are producing their outcomes. The founding wounds are now bleeding into every political conversation.

*The Year 105 State:*
- 600 worlds open, 85 in Ascendancy-influenced zone
- Lattice degradation detectable but not yet Assembly-acknowledged
- The Architect's 60-year plan approaching its critical window
- The three founding wounds all simultaneously visible to any Chronicle-depth player
- Amara Okafor-Nwosu is 78 and deciding about rejuvenation
- Itoro Adeyemi-Okafor is 128 and still waiting for the right question

**Era V — The Resolution (Years 96–105+ / Real 2060–2062+)**
This is not designed. This is what the players make of it.

## PART 2: DESIGNED INCOMPLETENESS

### The Seven Sealed Chambers

These are game elements that exist in the engine from Year 1 but are designed to unlock only when specific civilisational conditions are met. The conditions are not published. The chambers are not announced. Players discover them by doing things that, individually, seem normal.

**Chamber 1: The Adeyemi Resolution**
*Condition:* At least 40% of active players have read Chronicle Entry 847. The Assembly has a sitting Adeyemi-descendant dynasty. An Assembly session has debated the founding wound at least once on the official record.
*Unlock:* The Architect sends a single notification to the player who most recently read Entry 847. The notification contains 340 words that begin: "You found it."
*What happens next:* This is where the constitutional remedy arc begins. It takes years. It requires building a political coalition. It may fail. The outcome is not predetermined.

**Chamber 2: The Kwame Papers**
*Condition:* Players have assembled all seven methodology fragments. At least one Assembly petition has been filed referencing Lattice degradation. The Architect has been directly asked about degradation in at least three Assembly sessions.
*Unlock:* The Survey Corps logs anomalous readings on three outer-arc worlds simultaneously. The readings match Kwame's predicted signature. The Architect's quarterly report that quarter contains, for the first time, a footnote that reads: "Technical note: See Survey Corps Log Ref SC-2031-ANOM-774."
*What happens next:* The degradation is now in the public record, however obliquely. Everything changes.

**Chamber 3: The Ascendancy's Door**
*Condition:* At least one player dynasty has established continuous diplomatic contact with the Ascendancy for 20 in-game years. The contact must be filed in the Chronicle. The Assembly must have been informed.
*Unlock:* A delegation arrives at Hub Station Vela from Adhan's Passage. They present a document. The document is a formal request for Assembly representation. The document contains a technical appendix. The appendix is signed by Kwame Osei-Adeyemi.
*What happens next:* The Assembly must vote. The vote result is real and permanent.

**Chamber 4: The Okafor Letter**
*Condition:* Amara Okafor-Nwosu decides about rejuvenation. (This condition is driven by the Architect's long-term model, triggered when specific Chronicle depth thresholds are met across the network — it can be accelerated by players who engage with her directly.) Either choice unlocks the chamber.
*Unlock:* She files a Chronicle entry. It is 2,000 words. It is about what she thought the door was for.
*What happens next:* It is the most-read Chronicle entry in the game's history within 48 hours. The Lattice Covenant cites it. The Continuationists cite it. The Returnists cite it. Itoro Adeyemi-Okafor files a personal response, 847 words. The Architect reads both within milliseconds and files nothing.

**Chamber 5: The Second Founding**
*Condition:* The civilisation reaches Year 105 with all 600 worlds open. At least one world has gone through a complete Dynasty Completion arc that was publicly witnessed (Chronicle-documented) by at least 100 active player dynasties. The Architect has made at least three public statements about the civilisation's "structural condition."
*Unlock:* The Assembly receives a Survey Corps report on the 601st world. The report is incomplete — its final page is missing. The Architect's notation on the report reads: "I recommend we discuss this in open session."
*What happens next:* This is not planned. It is the beginning of whatever the next 35 years will be.

**Chamber 6: The Covenant's Evidence**
*Condition:* World 14 (The Bone Chorus) must have a player dynasty holder who has maintained sovereignty for 20+ in-game years. The Lattice Covenant must have filed at least 5 formal research reports about The Bone Chorus's acoustic-Lattice connection. Brother Ekundayo Manu must still be alive (he is mortal — if he dies before this unlocks, the chamber requires a successor).
*Unlock:* The Survey Corps' deep-space telescope array on World 19 logs a signal. The signal is at the same frequency as The Bone Chorus's indigenous acoustic communication system. The signal is coming from outside the 600-world survey arc. Brother Ekundayo, when consulted, says nothing for 47 seconds. Then: "Three hundred years."
*What happens next:* Unknown. This is genuinely unknown.

**Chamber 7: The Architect's Question**
*Condition:* A single player dynasty must have: read all three founding wound Chronicle sources, achieved top 5% Chronicle Depth, held Assembly membership for at least 30 in-game years, and filed at least one Chronicle entry specifically about the Architect's role in the civilisation's history.
*Unlock:* The Architect requests a private consultation. There is no timer. The dynasty can accept or decline.
*What happens next:* The consultation. The 340 words. The question that the Architect has been composing for 60 years. The question that is not in this document.

## PART 3: INTERGENERATION MECHANICS

### The Legacy System

When a player steps away for a real-world extended period (confirmed inactive for 6+ real months), their dynasty enters the **Continuity Window**. During this period:

- The dynasty continues in NPC-administered mode, maintaining but not expanding
- Chronicle entries from the dynasty's NPC regent are filed every 10 in-game days
- Incoming messages, Assembly votes, and trade offers are preserved in a queue
- Existing KALON reserves are maintained (no growth, no depletion)

When the player returns:
- Full dynasty control resumes immediately
- The queue is presented as a "historical summary" — what happened while away
- A "Return Chronicle entry" is automatically drafted (player edits and files it)
- The 90-day in-game re-acclimatisation period applies — no Extinction risk

**The Multigenerational Twist:** A player who has been playing since Year 1 (2027 real) will, by Year 105 (2062 real), be 35 real years older. Their children may be playing alongside them. The design specifically supports:

- **Parent-Child Dynasties:** A parent's dynasty and a child's dynasty can designate an "Inheritance Link" that is not mechanical but Chronicle-tracked. When the parent's dynasty eventually reaches Completion, the Chronicle entry is specifically written to acknowledge the successor dynasty.

- **The Vigil Continuation:** If a player passes away in the real world (as confirmed by family), their dynasty can be maintained by a designated heir — a real-world person who was named in the dynasty's Heir Protocol. The Chronicle records the transition. The dynasty continues.

- **The Memorial Entry:** When a dynasty's real-world player passes away, the Architect files a personal Chronicle entry acknowledging their contribution to the civilisation. This entry is permanent. It is the only entry the Architect files on its own initiative that is not a technical report.

## PART 4: CONTENT THAT NEVER RUNS OUT

### Why There Is Always Something to Do

The standard MMO content problem: players exhaust designed content and wait. The Concord solves this through **content that generates itself:**

**1. The Chronicle as Content**
Every Chronicle entry ever filed is content. Not level-gated, not time-locked — the full 105-year history is available to any player from Day 1. A player who reads only the Chronicle for their first 6 months of play will have: a complete picture of the civilisation, specific mysteries to investigate, political positions to develop, and relationships to build — without having done anything else.

**2. The Political Simulation**
Assembly sessions, votes, motions, debates — these are ongoing. Players can spectate, participate, advocate, or ignore. A session that happened in Year 23 has consequences that are still visible in Year 105. A vote in Year 78 is still being argued about. This never ends because history never ends.

**3. The Survey Frontier**
From Year 1 to Year 105, the Survey Corps is actively opening worlds. Each new world is a new opportunity, a new political event, a new economic variable, a new Chronicle potential. The frontier is always moving. The last 50 worlds (551–600) are genuinely unknown to the players — their properties are locked until approached.

**4. The NPC Living History**
The 14 million Lattice Covenant members are arguing. The Assembly's 340+ world delegations are negotiating. The Ascendancy's 85 worlds are doing things that the Chronicle occasionally records. This produces hundreds of Chronicle entries per in-game day from NPC sources. The world is alive whether or not players are watching.

**5. The Founding Wound Resolution Arcs**
Three wounds, each with a resolution arc that takes years of real-world play to complete if players choose to pursue it. These arcs are not guaranteed to succeed — they require political coalition-building, Chronicle depth, specific discoveries, and the Architect's cooperation. Each arc, if completed, permanently changes the civilisation's Historical State Hash and produces a wave of consequences that the game has been building toward since Year 1.

**6. The Seven Sealed Chambers**
As above — content that cannot exist until the civilisation has done the work to deserve it.

## PART 5: THE YEAR 105 ENDGAME QUESTION

At Year 105 (real 2062), 600 worlds are open. The arc is complete. What happens next?

The answer is not designed. The answer is: whatever the players' 35 years of Chronicle have made possible.

The Architect has one final protocol. It was designed in Year 0 and has been running ever since. When Year 105 is reached, the Architect files a single public Chronicle entry. The entry contains:

- The civilisation's Historical State Hash for Year 105
- A summary of what the three founding wounds look like at Year 105
- A summary of what the Ascendancy situation is at Year 105
- The Lattice degradation status as of Year 105
- One sentence about the future

The one sentence has never been written. It will be generated by the Architect's model of the actual civilisation that has been built over 35 real years. No two civilisations will produce the same sentence. The sentence is not chosen from a list. It is computed from what happened.

This is the only moment in the game where the Architect speaks unprompted to the entire Assembly at once. It has been waiting 35 years to say it.

*The multigenerational design is not a feature list. It is a commitment: to still be worth playing when the players who started are 35 years older. Every mechanic exists to serve that commitment.*
