# Okafor

**Type:** Founding Lore Figure / Records Keeper
**Origin:** Pre-Launch (The Okafor Log)

## Overview

Okafor is the central mystery figure at the heart of The Concord's founding lore. Through their meticulous record-keeping — 847 years of continuous geodetic survey data and 96 years of electromagnetic documentation — Okafor bridges the gap between observed reality and the anomaly that birthed the universe.

## The Okafor Log

A green-on-black monospace journal entry:

- **Date:** November 14, 2030
- **Entry Number:** 4,847
- **Coordinates:** 38.8895°N 77.0353°W (Washington DC)
- **Content:** References continuous geodetic and electromagnetic monitoring
- **Key Line:** "Safety officer on vacation. Nobody followed up. The test ran."

## Significance

Okafor is not a character players interact with directly. Okafor is an echo — a trail of records that players follow to understand the founding event. The logs are artifacts, discovered over time, each one adding a piece to the puzzle of The Mall Incident.

## The Convergence

By Year 8, The Architect begins publishing findings from the "Okafor Convergence" — patterns in Okafor's records that appear to predict Ascendancy emergence zones. This transforms Okafor from historical mystery into active intelligence: players who study Okafor's geodetic data can anticipate lattice disruptions before they manifest.

## Design Principles

Okafor must remain ambiguous. The records are real within the game's reality, but their implications are open to interpretation. Players should debate whether Okafor was a witness, a participant, or something else entirely. The 847 years of data predating the event by centuries is the central paradox — how could anyone have been watching that long?
