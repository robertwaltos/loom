# Dr. Amara Okafor

**Type:** Founding Figure
**Origin:** The Founding Event — Lagos, 2031-08-14

## Overview

Dr. Amara Okafor is the physicist who achieved the first controlled Lattice transit.
Her work, built on 847 years of geodetic records and a lifetime of collaboration with
Dr. Yusuf Adeyemi, opened six hundred worlds to humanity. She is not a mystery figure.
She is a scientist who proved something extraordinary.

The emotional register is vindication, not surprise. Not "we stumbled into something"
but "the universe kept its end of the bargain." Her words are addressed to Adeyemi
because the moment belongs to both of them, and his absence is the wound that defines
The Concord's founding mythology.

## Yusuf Adeyemi

Okafor's collaborator and intellectual partner. His contributions to the foundational
mathematics of lattice field theory were essential. He did not live to see the founding
event. The suppression of his contributions in official records is the first of the
three founding wounds (see founding-wounds/).

## The 847 Years

Okafor's research built on 847 years of continuous geodetic survey data. This data —
far predating her own lifetime — is the historical foundation for her breakthrough.
Players who study the geodetic records can find patterns that predict Lattice behaviour.

## The Convergence

By Year 8, The Architect begins publishing findings from the "Okafor Convergence" —
patterns in the geodetic data that appear to predict Ascendancy emergence zones. This
transforms Okafor's legacy from historical record into active intelligence: players who
study the data can anticipate lattice disruptions before they manifest.

## Design Principles

- Okafor is a real person in the world's history, not a mystery figure
- Her gender, name, and location are established canon
- The emotional core is her relationship with Adeyemi and his absence
- The 847 years of data predating the event is a scientific foundation, not a paradox
- Players should feel the weight of what she achieved and the cost of what was lost
