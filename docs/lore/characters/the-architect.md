# The Architect

**Type:** Singular Meta-Entity / Developer Voice
**Presence:** From Day Zero

## Overview

The Architect is the in-world manifestation of developer transparency. Not an NPC in the traditional sense — The Architect is a role, a perspective, a presence that bridges the gap between the development team and the living world. Comparable to Path of Exile's transparent developer communication, but embedded directly into the game's reality.

## Authority

- **Economic:** Controls the Architect's Reserve (20M KALON, 2% of total initial supply)
- **Constitutional:** Advisory vote in Assembly governance (7-14% weight depending on vote type)
- **Narrative:** Publishes reports that are simultaneously developer updates and in-world lore

## The Architect's Agents (Tier 4 NPCs)

10-50 canonical characters across all worlds. These are the highest-tier AI entities in The Concord:

- Powered by Claude Opus
- Drive world-scale narrative events
- Named and permanently recorded in The Chronicle
- Each has full character history, relationships, and goals
- Persistent memory across all interactions

## Reports

- **Year Zero Report:** Published at launch, establishing the initial state of the universe
- **Quarterly Reports:** Ongoing economic analysis, lattice health assessments, population dynamics
- **The Okafor Convergence:** Ongoing investigation into the patterns surrounding the founding event (see: founding-wounds/the-mall-incident.md)

## Design Principles

The Architect never lies, but speaks in incomplete truths. The Architect never intervenes directly (Article IX: No Cheat Codes — developers cannot reset world state). The Architect observes, reports, and occasionally steers attention toward patterns that players might otherwise miss.

## Connection to Okafor

The Architect maintains incomplete findings on the Okafor Convergence. This investigation becomes player-visible by Year 8, when Ascendancy activity reaches civilisational scale. The Architect's role shifts from observer to active investigator, drawing players into a meta-narrative that connects the founding wound to current events.
