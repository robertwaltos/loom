# World Types

**Total Worlds:** 600
**Unlock Timeline:** ~35 real years via Survey Corps exploration

## Classification

| Type         | Count | Description                                                 |
| ------------ | ----- | ----------------------------------------------------------- |
| Garden       | ~150  | Earth-like biomes, high habitability, strong KALON issuance |
| Arid         | ~100  | Desert worlds, scarce water, resource extraction economies  |
| Ocean        | ~75   | Water-dominant worlds, archipelago civilisations            |
| Frozen       | ~75   | Ice worlds, extreme conditions, hardy populations           |
| Volcanic     | ~60   | Geologically active, mineral-rich, dangerous terrain        |
| Ancient      | ~60   | Pre-existing ruins, archaeological mystery worlds           |
| Eden         | ~65   | Paradisiacal worlds, highest habitability scores            |
| Void-Touched | 15    | Ascendancy-influenced, modified physics, 3% of total        |

## Void-Touched Worlds

The 15 Void-Touched worlds are the physical manifestation of Ascendancy influence. They exhibit:

- Modified physics constants within their boundaries
- Corrupted lattice node signatures
- Degraded beacon integrity (often starting at `compromised`)
- Reduced KALON issuance due to low lattice integrity scores
- Unique flora, fauna, and environmental hazards

Void-Touched worlds are not inherently hostile — they are different. Players who master their modified physics gain strategic advantages that are unavailable on standard worlds.

## Stellar Standard Impact

Each world's KALON issuance is determined by its physical properties via the Stellar Standard formula:

```
annualIssuance = BASE × stellarClassMultiplier × zoneMultiplier × nodeDensity × integrity / 100
```

World type influences stellar class and orbital zone placement, creating natural economic variation. Garden and Eden worlds in habitable zones of G-class stars produce the highest issuance. Void-Touched worlds with degraded integrity produce the lowest.

## Survey Corps

New worlds are discovered by the Survey Corps — player explorers who venture into uncharted lattice space. Discovery unlocks happen at a pace designed to sustain the 35-year content horizon. Each new world adds economic capacity, narrative threads, and territorial dynamics to the living universe.
