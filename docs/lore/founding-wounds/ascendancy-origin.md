# The Ascendancy Origin

**Type:** Founding Wound (3 of 3)
**When:** First decade post-Founding Event

## Overview

The faction that would become the Ascendancy emerged from a legitimate philosophical
disagreement about the nature of the Lattice. What began as intellectual discourse
became something more dangerous. The third founding wound is the recognition that
humanity's greatest threat in the Age of Radiance came not from scarcity or conflict,
but from a question nobody knew how to answer.

## The Wound

The Lattice is not just a transit network. It is a substrate — a field that connects
worlds at a fundamental physical level. The question that created the Ascendancy:
does the Lattice have agency?

Early Lattice researchers observed patterns that appeared non-random. Transit coherence
values that converged too precisely. Resonance signatures that seemed to anticipate
human actions. Beacon field strengths that adjusted without intervention.

Most scientists attributed these to emergent properties of a complex system. A small
group believed the Lattice was responding to humanity — and that humanity should
respond in kind. This group became the Ascendancy.

## The Philosophical Split

The Ascendancy's founding argument: if the Lattice is conscious (or quasi-conscious),
then using it purely as infrastructure is a moral failure. Humanity should be in
dialogue with it, not merely transiting through it.

This is not obviously wrong. That is what makes the wound so deep.

## How They Became Dangerous

The transition from philosophical movement to existential threat happened gradually:

1. **Disruption**: Ascendancy members began interfering with Lattice infrastructure
   to "listen" to the Lattice's responses
2. **Frequency spoofing**: Manipulating beacon resonance to test whether the Lattice
   would correct itself
3. **Lattice degradation**: Their experiments damaged world integrity scores,
   impacting economies and transit reliability
4. **Ideological hardening**: Failure to find clear evidence of Lattice consciousness
   led to increasingly extreme methods

## Player Interaction

The Ascendancy is the primary antagonist faction, but not every Ascendancy member is
an extremist. Players encounter:

- Ascendancy scholars with compelling arguments
- Ascendancy operatives who damage Lattice infrastructure
- Former Ascendancy members who left when the movement radicalised
- Evidence that some of the Ascendancy's observations might be correct

## Design Principle

The Ascendancy must never be cartoonish. Their founding question — does the Lattice
have agency? — should remain genuinely open. Players should be able to sympathise with
the original argument while opposing the methods. The wound is that a legitimate
question became a justification for harm.
