# The Adeyemi Suppression

**Type:** Founding Wound (1 of 3)
**When:** Post-Founding Event, 2031 onwards

## Overview

Dr. Yusuf Adeyemi's contributions to the foundational mathematics of lattice field
theory were essential to Dr. Amara Okafor's breakthrough. He did not live to see the
founding event on 2031-08-14. In the aftermath, official records minimised his role.
This is the first political failure of the Age of Radiance.

## The Wound

The suppression was not a conspiracy. It was the mundane machinery of credit and
attribution failing in the face of an extraordinary event. Okafor's name became
synonymous with the founding. Adeyemi's was footnoted.

Okafor herself protested this. Her final words in the founding log — "Yusuf. It works.
I wish you were here to see this. I think you always knew it would." — are addressed
to him. But institutions move slowly, and the narrative of a single genius is easier
to tell than a story of collaboration.

## Player Discovery

Players discover the full truth through Chronicle entries and lore fragments scattered
across the founding worlds. The Adeyemi mathematical proofs are embedded in Lattice
field equations that players encounter during transit. Attentive players recognise that
the mathematics pre-dates the founding and traces to a different hand.

## Faction Response

- **Continuationists** seek formal recognition and amendment of the historical record
- **Returnists** use the suppression as evidence that the Age of Radiance replicates
  old power structures
- **The Ascendancy** is indifferent — they consider the founding event irrelevant to
  their goals

## Design Principle

The suppression should feel uncomfortably real. It is not a villain's scheme. It is
the kind of injustice that happens in every era, even ages of abundance. Players should
feel that correcting it matters — that the Chronicle's permanence is the antidote to
institutional amnesia.
