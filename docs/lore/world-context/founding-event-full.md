# The Founding Event — Complete Record

> 2031-08-14, Lagos. Dr. Amara Okafor achieves Lattice transit.

## The Principals

**Dr. Amara Okafor** — Theoretical physicist. Pioneer of lattice field theory. The first
human to achieve controlled transit between two points in spacetime using the Lattice.
Her work built on 847 years of geodetic records and a lifetime of collaboration with
Yusuf Adeyemi.

**Dr. Yusuf Adeyemi** — Okafor's collaborator and intellectual partner. His contributions
to the foundational mathematics of lattice field theory were essential. He did not live
to see the founding event. His absence is one of the three founding wounds of The Concord.

## The Emotional Register

The ending is vindication, not surprise. Not "we stumbled into something" but "the
universe kept its end of the bargain." Okafor's words are addressed to Adeyemi because
the moment belongs to both of them, and his absence is the wound that defines The Concord's
founding mythology.

## The Three Founding Wounds

1. **The Adeyemi Suppression**: How Adeyemi's contributions were minimised in official
   records. The first political failure of the age of abundance. Players discover the
   full truth through Chronicle entries and lore fragments.

2. **The Launch World Selection**: The process by which the first Lattice destination
   was chosen. Political, not scientific. The tensions it created persist.

3. **The Ascendancy Origin**: How the faction that would become the Ascendancy emerged
   from a legitimate philosophical disagreement about the nature of the Lattice.

## What This Means for The Chronicle

Chronicle Entry 1 is the founding event log above. Every subsequent entry in every
player's Chronicle descends from this moment. When a player writes "Build your
Chronicle," they are adding to a chain that begins with Okafor's words in Lagos.
