# How To Write Well — A Guide to Your Chronicle

> Tone: invitation, not instruction. This is for players.

## Your Chronicle Writes Itself (Mostly)

Every significant action you take in The Concord generates a Chronicle entry
automatically. Found a dynasty? Recorded. Settle a new world? Recorded. Win a vote
in the Assembly? Recorded. Survive a crisis that nearly ended your dynasty? Recorded.

You do not need to write anything. The Concord writes for you.

But you can write better.

## What Makes a Chronicle Deep

Chronicle Depth is one of three components of your Civic Score (the others being
Economic Standing and Civic Contribution). Depth is not about quantity. A dynasty
with a thousand trivial entries has less depth than one with fifty significant ones.

What generates depth:

- **Founding moments**: Starting something new. A dynasty, an alliance, a settlement.
- **Crisis survival**: Your dynasty faced completion and came back.
- **World transitions**: Moving between worlds via the Lattice.
- **Governance**: Proposing motions in the Assembly. Voting on significant decisions.
- **Economic impact**: Transactions that meaningfully change the balance of a world.
- **Memorial entries**: Writing the final Chronicle entry for a completing dynasty.

What does not generate depth:

- Repetitive actions (grinding the same trade route)
- Trivial transactions (buying and selling small amounts)
- Passive presence (logging in but doing nothing)

## The Memorial Entry

The most significant Chronicle entry a player can write is a memorial entry for
another dynasty that has completed. During the Estate Dispersal Auction, one of the
lots is always a Memorial Bid. The winning bidder writes the final Chronicle entry
for the completing dynasty.

This entry is permanent. It is the last thing that dynasty's Chronicle will ever say.
Write it well.

## The Invitation

Your Chronicle is yours. It belongs to you and to the world you are building.
It is not a task to be optimised. It is not a score to be maximised. It is the
record of what you chose to do with your time in the most extraordinary moment
in human history.

Six hundred worlds are waiting. Your story starts now.
