The Permanence Covenant

A promise made to every player. Forever.

The Concord is not a game with an ending. It is a civilisation with a memory. Everything that happens inside it ΓÇö every world founded, every dynasty built, every sacrifice recorded ΓÇö is committed permanently, publicly, and irrevocably. These are not marketing promises. The Full Document

The Permanence Covenant

The following is the complete and ratified text of the Permanence Covenant. It is reproduced here in full and without modification. The canonical version is recorded on-chain at the address below.

The Concord ΓÇö Permanence Covenant ΓÇö Canonical Document ΓÇö Version 1.0 ΓÇö Ratified Year Zero

We, the founders of The Concord, make this Covenant with every person who chooses to build a life within it. A civilisation is nothing without its memory. A game is nothing without the trust of those who play it. We do not ask for your trust. We make it structurally impossible to betray it. The following provisions are not aspirational. They are technical commitments, legally binding, enforced by cryptographic architecture, witnessed by the community, and irrevocable from the moment of ratification.

The World's History Is Permanent
Nothing that has happened in The Concord will ever be erased, retconned, or reset. A world founded in Year Zero remains founded in Year Zero. A dynasty that defended a Lattice gate remains in the Remembrance as its defenders for as long as any infrastructure exists to serve the record. There are no server wipes. There is no rollback. The Concord has no undo.
Dynasty NFTs Are Permanent
Every significant achievement ΓÇö First Contact, World Founding, Gate Defence, Ascendancy Survival ΓÇö is minted as a Dynasty NFT on the Concord Chain. If the game ever shuts down, a migration protocol activates automatically, moving all Dynasty NFTs to a public Ethereum contract where they remain as historical artefacts in perpetuity. No action required from the player. The contract is already deployed.
The Remembrance Is Open to All
The full historical record of The Concord ΓÇö every settled world, every founding declaration, every recorded death, every dynasty lineage, every Covenant violation ΓÇö is publicly readable without a subscription, without an account, without any barrier whatsoever. You do not need to play The Concord to read its history. The Remembrance is not a feature. It is humanity's record of its second civilisation.
The Source Code Returns to the Commons
If The Concord ever fully shuts down ΓÇö all servers offline, all development ceased ΓÇö the complete engine source code is released to the community under an open licence within 30 days. The community may run the Concord on its own infrastructure. The world does not die because the company does. This commitment is irrevocable.
The Assembly Continues
If all human operators withdraw from The Concord, the Assembly ΓÇö The Concord's governance body ΓÇö shall continue under AI stewardship, maintaining the world's rules and precedents as established by the player community. Governance shall not cease because the studio ceases.
Player Dynasties Are Protected
No player dynasty shall be deleted, forcibly merged, or structurally altered without the express written consent of the player who founded it, or their designated heir. Dynasty records persist in the Remembrance regardless of account status, subscription lapse, or game shutdown. Your legacy belongs to you.
The Architect's Accountability
The Architect ΓÇö the studio's lead custodian ΓÇö shall publish a public quarterly report on the state of all Covenant provisions: compliance status, any stress indicators, and any actions taken to preserve the Covenant's integrity. These reports shall remain permanently publicly accessible, archived in the Remembrance and on-chain.
"You are not renting a world. You are not leasing a legacy. What you build in The Concord belongs to you ΓÇö and to history ΓÇö for as long as there are humans to remember it."

The Seven Provisions

What The Covenant Guarantees

Seven structural guarantees. Each enforced by architecture, contract, and cryptographic proof. Not by the goodwill of a company.

Provision I
The Chronicle Shall Not Be Deleted
The full historical record of The Concord ΓÇö every event, every death, every dynasty, every world founded ΓÇö is committed to an append-only hash chain. Not even the studio can rewrite confirmed history. The Remembrance does not have an erase function.
Provision II
The MARKS Shall Remain With Their Holders
Dynasty NFTs and achievement MARKS live in player wallets on a public chain. If every Concord server goes dark tonight, every player wakes up tomorrow with their MARKS intact, unaffected, and permanently readable by any Ethereum-compatible explorer.
Provision III
The Source Code Shall Be Released If The Studio Ceases Operation
If The Concord Studio ceases operation permanently, the full engine source is released under Apache 2.0 within 30 days. A migration bond held in independent escrow executes automatically if this deadline is missed. The community can run The Concord without us.
Provision IV
The Assembly Shall Continue With AI Governance If All Humans Withdraw
The governance of The Concord ΓÇö its laws, its precedents, its Assembly ΓÇö continues under AI stewardship if the studio fully withdraws. The world's rules are not stored in a human mind or a company policy document. They are encoded in the Covenant itself.
Provision V
The Founding Families' Historical Advantage Shall Be Documented Permanently
Early players hold genuine historical advantage: earlier founding dates, rarer MARKS, deeper Remembrance records. This is acknowledged openly and recorded permanently. The Concord does not pretend the past is neutral. First is first. The Remembrance remembers.
Provision VI
Player Dynasties Shall Not Be Deleted Without Player Consent
No dynasty is erased. An absent player's dynasty enters Legacy status ΓÇö AI-governed, historically preserved, still part of the world. Deletion requires the express consent of the founding player or their designated heir. The Remembrance holds the record regardless.
Provision VII
The Architect's Quarterly Reports Shall Remain Public Permanently
Every 90 days, the Architect publishes the Covenant Compliance Report: current status of all seven provisions, any stress indicators detected, and any corrective actions taken. These reports are archived in the Remembrance and recorded on-chain. They cannot be deleted. You can read the first report from Year Zero in the same place you read today's report: permanently.

Article V ┬╖ The Succession Doctrine

On Absence and Legacy

The Concord exists in real time. Players live, play, pause, and return. The world continues whether they are present or not. The Succession Doctrine governs what happens to a dynasty during its player's absence.

Article V ΓÇö Doctrine of Digital Mortality
On the Nature of Digital Mortality

The Concord does not pretend that absence is neutral. In a civilisation where time moves forward and history accumulates, choosing not to participate has consequences ΓÇö as it does in every civilisation that has ever existed. The Digital Mortality Protocol codifies this truth with precision and without cruelty.

When a player's account lapses beyond 90 days without payment or activity, the following sequence activates:

  • 01 The player's active character is formally recorded as Deceased in the Remembrance ΓÇö their name, their dynasty's history, their last known location and action, all committed to the permanent record with a date of departure.
  • 02 All personal liquid assets ΓÇö CONCORDIUM holdings, undeployed credits, personal inventory items ΓÇö are dissolved into the game's Universal Prosperity Allocation pool, distributed as a fractional increase across all active citizens. The wealth of the departed becomes the sustenance of the living.
  • 03 World Sovereignty, if held, passes to the established Regent under Legacy Dynasty protocol. The dynasty continues. The sovereign line continues. Only the player is gone.
  • 04 The player's Dynasty NFTs remain in their wallet ΓÇö permanent, untouched, theirs forever. Digital death does not erase legacy. The Remembrance keeps the record regardless of account status.
  • 05 If the player returns and creates a new account, they begin again. A new character. A new family. A new beginning. The old dynasty exists in the Remembrance as history ΓÇö legible, honourable, complete. It cannot be reclaimed. It was its own story. This one is new.

"In the Concord, as in life: what you built matters. What you left behind matters. And who comes after you ΓÇö that matters most of all."

Formal Terms of Service Language

The Concord ΓÇö Terms of Service ΓÇö Permanence Provisions (Schedule C)
┬º C.1 ΓÇö World History Immutability
The operator warrants that no game event, player action, or world history entry confirmed and recorded in the Remembrance shall be retroactively altered, deleted, or recontextualised by any party, including the operator, without the affirmative consent of a supermajority (67%) of the Player Council. Operator may not unilaterally rewrite confirmed history. Emergency corrections for technical error are permitted within 48 hours of recording only, subject to public disclosure.
┬º C.2 ΓÇö Dynasty NFT Migration Guarantee
In the event of permanent service termination, operator shall execute the NFT Migration Protocol within 30 calendar days, transferring all registered Dynasty NFTs to the public Ethereum migration contract. This obligation survives corporate dissolution and is secured by a migration bond held in independent escrow. Failure to migrate triggers automatic bond execution.
┬º C.3 ΓÇö Remembrance Public Access
The Remembrance API shall remain publicly accessible without authentication or fee for a minimum of 10 years following any service termination, funded by the Remembrance Preservation Reserve (minimum 2% of gross revenue annually). Access includes full read access to all historical records, dynasty lineages, world histories, and Covenant violation logs.
┬º C.4 ΓÇö Source Code Release Obligation
Upon permanent service termination, operator shall release the complete engine source code under the Apache 2.0 open-source licence within 30 calendar days. Release shall include sufficient documentation for a technically competent third party to operate a functional instance of The Concord. This obligation is irrevocable.
┬º C.5 ΓÇö Digital Mortality Protocol (Account Lapse)
Accounts with no payment activity for 90 consecutive calendar days shall be processed under the Digital Mortality Protocol: (a) the active character record shall be updated to Deceased status in the Remembrance; (b) all liquid in-game assets shall be dissolved into the UPA distribution pool; (c) World Sovereignty transfers to Legacy Dynasty Regent status; (d) Dynasty NFTs are not affected and remain in the player's registered wallet; (e) upon account reactivation after lapse, the player must create a new character and dynasty. Players wishing to preserve active status during temporary absence may activate the Hibernation Protocol for periods up to 24 months.
┬º C.6 ΓÇö Player Council Oversight
A Player Council of 12 elected representatives holds co-signing authority over the Dynasty NFT migration contract and the Remembrance archive keys. Operator cannot unilaterally execute migration or archive deletion without Player Council co-signature. Player Council elections are held annually. Voting is open to all players with accounts in good standing for 6+ months.
